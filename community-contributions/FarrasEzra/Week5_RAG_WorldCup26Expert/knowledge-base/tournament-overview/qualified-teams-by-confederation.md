# 2026 FIFA World Cup — The 48 Qualified Teams by Confederation

The three host nations (Canada, Mexico, United States) qualified
automatically. The other 45 teams qualified through their confederations
or the intercontinental play-offs.

## Hosts (3)
Canada, Mexico, United States

## AFC — Asia (8 slots)
Australia, Iraq, IR Iran, Japan, Jordan, Korea Republic (South Korea),
Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Uzbekistan

## CAF — Africa (9 slots)
Algeria, Cabo Verde (Cape Verde), Congo DR (DR Congo), Côte d'Ivoire
(Ivory Coast), Egypt, Ghana, Morocco, Senegal, South Africa, Tunisia

## CONCACAF — North/Central America & Caribbean (non-host slots: 3)
Curaçao, Haiti, Panama (in addition to hosts Canada, Mexico, USA)

## CONMEBOL — South America (6 slots)
Argentina, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, Paraguay, Uruguay

## OFC — Oceania (1 slot)
New Zealand

## UEFA — Europe (16 slots)
Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Czechia (Czech
Republic), England, France, Germany, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal,
Scotland, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Türkiye (Turkey)

## Squad size
A record 1,248 players representing 48 nations were confirmed on June 2,
2026, when final squad lists were submitted to FIFA.

## Qualification notes
- Of the 48 qualified teams, 26 also appeared at the 2022 World Cup.
- Cape Verde, Curaçao, Jordan, and Uzbekistan made their World Cup debuts.
- New Zealand was the lowest-ranked qualifier (85th in the world).
- South Africa returned after its last appearance as 2010 host.
- Two intercontinental playoff spots were decided in Mexico in late March
  2026, contested by one team from each confederation (excluding UEFA)
  plus an extra CONCACAF team.
- Four UEFA teams qualified via a second-round playoff path; the last
  European berths (including Bosnia and Herzegovina's win over Italy on
  penalties) were only confirmed shortly before the December 2025 draw.
