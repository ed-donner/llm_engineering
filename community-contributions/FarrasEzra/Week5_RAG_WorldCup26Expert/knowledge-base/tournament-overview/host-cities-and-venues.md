## Key facts
- The USA hosts the most matches (78 total), including every match from
  the quarterfinal stage onward. Mexico and Canada each host 13 matches
  (group stage and early knockout rounds only).
- Estadio Azteca (Mexico City) is the only stadium in the tournament that
  has previously hosted World Cup matches, having done so in both 1970
  and 1986.
- The opening match was Mexico vs. South Africa on June 11, 2026, at
  Estadio Azteca.
- The final is at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, on
  July 19, 2026.
- Host cities are grouped into three regions for scheduling: Western
  (Vancouver, Seattle, San Francisco, Los Angeles), Central (Guadalajara,
  Mexico City, Monterrey, Houston, Dallas, Kansas City), and Eastern
  (Atlanta, Miami, Toronto, Boston, Philadelphia, New York/New Jersey).
- Each host nation played all three of its group-stage matches on home
  soil: Mexico (Group A), Canada (Group B), and the United States
  (Group D).
