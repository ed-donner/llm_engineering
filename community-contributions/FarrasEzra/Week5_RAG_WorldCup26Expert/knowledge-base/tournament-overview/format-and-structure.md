# 2026 FIFA World Cup — Format and Structure

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the 23rd edition of the tournament and the current
edition as of July 2026. It runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026.

## Hosts
It is jointly hosted by three countries for the first time in World Cup
history: the United States, Mexico, and Canada. Matches are played across
16 host cities: 11 in the United States, 3 in Mexico, and 2 in Canada.

- Mexico previously hosted in 1970 and 1986, becoming the first country to
  host or co-host the World Cup three times.
- The United States previously hosted in 1994.
- This is Canada's first time hosting or co-hosting a men's World Cup.

## Expansion to 48 teams
This is the first World Cup with 48 teams, up from 32 in every edition from
1998 to 2022. It is the first format change since 1998. The 48 teams are
split into 12 groups of four (Groups A through L).

- Each team plays 3 group-stage matches (round-robin within its group).
- The top 2 teams from each group advance automatically to the knockout
  stage (24 teams).
- The 8 best third-placed teams across all 12 groups also advance
  (8 teams), bringing the Round of 32 field to 32 teams total.
- From the Round of 32 onward, it is single-elimination.

## Match totals
- Total matches: 104 (up from 64 in 2022) — 72 in the group stage and
  32 in the knockout stage.
- Tournament length: 39 days (compared to 29 days for Qatar 2022 and
  32 days for 2014/2018), the longest World Cup ever.

## Knockout stage rules
If scores are level after 90 minutes in the knockout stage, 30 minutes of
extra time is played. If still tied, the match is decided by a penalty
shoot-out. A mandatory three-minute hydration break is held in each half
of every match due to heat conditions in several host cities.

## Schedule by round
- Group stage Matchday 1: June 11–17, 2026
- Group stage Matchday 2: June 18–23, 2026
- Group stage Matchday 3: June 24–27, 2026
- Round of 32: June 28 – July 3, 2026
- Round of 16: July 4–7, 2026
- Quarterfinals: July 9–11, 2026
- Semifinals: July 14–15, 2026
- Third-place match: July 18, 2026
- Final: July 19, 2026, at MetLife Stadium (New York New Jersey Stadium),
  East Rutherford, New Jersey

## Defending champion
Argentina enters as the defending champion, having won the 2022 World Cup
in Qatar on penalties against France.

## Notable firsts and milestones
- Cape Verde, Curaçao, Jordan, and Uzbekistan are making their World Cup
  debuts.
- Qatar qualified for the first time on merit (its only prior appearance
  was as 2022 host).
- DR Congo and Haiti returned after their only previous appearance in 1974.
- Iraq returned after its only previous appearance in 1986.
- Austria, Norway, and Scotland returned after last appearing in 1998.
- Curaçao is the smallest and least populous nation to ever qualify.
- Italy (four-time champion) missed out for a third consecutive World Cup,
  losing the UEFA playoff final to Bosnia and Herzegovina on penalties.
- Other notable teams that did not qualify: Costa Rica, Cameroon, Denmark,
  Poland, Serbia, Wales.
