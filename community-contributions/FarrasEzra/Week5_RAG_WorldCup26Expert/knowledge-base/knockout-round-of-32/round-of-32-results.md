# 2026 FIFA World Cup — Round of 32 Results

The Round of 32 was played June 28 – July 3, 2026, the tournament's
newly introduced knockout round (a first for the World Cup, created by
the expansion to 48 teams). All 16 matches below were single-elimination;
matches level after 90 minutes went to extra time and then penalties if
still tied.

## Sunday, June 28
- Canada 1–0 South Africa (Los Angeles) → Canada advances to face Morocco

## Monday, June 29
- Brazil 2–1 Japan (Houston) → Brazil advances to face Norway
- Paraguay 1–1 Germany, Paraguay won 4–3 on penalties (Boston) →
  Paraguay advances to face France
- Morocco 1–1 Netherlands, Morocco won 3–2 on penalties (Monterrey) →
  Morocco advances to face Canada

## Tuesday, June 30
- Norway 2–1 Ivory Coast (Dallas) → Norway advances to face Brazil
- France 3–0 Sweden (East Rutherford, NJ) → France advances to face
  Paraguay
- Mexico 2–0 Ecuador (Mexico City) → Mexico advances to face England

## Wednesday, July 1
- England 2–1 DR Congo (Atlanta) → England advances to face Mexico
- Belgium 3–2 Senegal (Seattle) → Belgium advances to face USA
- USA 2–0 Bosnia and Herzegovina (Santa Clara) → USA advances to face
  Belgium

## Thursday, July 2
- Spain 3–0 Austria (Los Angeles) → Spain advances to face Portugal
- Portugal 2–1 Croatia (Toronto) → Portugal advances to face Spain
- Switzerland 2–0 Algeria (Vancouver) → Switzerland advances to face
  Colombia

## Friday, July 3
- Egypt 1–1 Australia, Egypt won 4–2 on penalties (Dallas) → Egypt
  advances to face Argentina
- Argentina 3–2 Cape Verde (Miami) → Argentina advances to face Egypt
- Colombia 1–0 Ghana (Kansas City) → Colombia advances to face
  Switzerland

## Teams eliminated in the Round of 32
South Africa, Japan, Germany, Netherlands, Ivory Coast, Sweden, Ecuador,
DR Congo, Senegal, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Austria, Croatia, Algeria,
Australia, Cape Verde, Ghana

## The 16 teams that advanced to the Round of 16
Canada, Brazil, Paraguay, Morocco, Norway, France, Mexico, England,
Belgium, USA, Spain, Portugal, Switzerland, Egypt, Argentina, Colombia
