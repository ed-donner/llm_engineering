# 2026 FIFA World Cup Qualification — Overview

Qualification for the 48-team tournament ran across all six FIFA
confederations from 2023 through March 2026, the first World Cup cycle
in which every confederation was guaranteed at least one berth (the
first time this has happened since the 2010 cycle).

## Slot allocation by confederation
- AFC (Asia): 8 direct slots
- CAF (Africa): 9 direct slots
- CONCACAF (North/Central America & Caribbean): 3 direct slots + 3 hosts
  (Canada, Mexico, USA) automatically qualified
- CONMEBOL (South America): 6 direct slots
- OFC (Oceania): 1 direct slot (first-ever guaranteed OFC slot)
- UEFA (Europe): 16 direct slots
- Intercontinental play-offs: 2 additional slots decided among 6 teams
  (one from each confederation other than UEFA, plus an extra CONCACAF
  team), seeded and played as a knockout mini-tournament hosted in
  Mexico in late March 2026 as a test event for the World Cup.

## Notable qualification storylines
- Italy, a four-time champion, lost the UEFA playoff final on penalties
  to Bosnia and Herzegovina, missing a third consecutive World Cup — the
  first time a former champion has missed three straight tournaments.
- Costa Rica failed to qualify for the first time since 2010.
- Cameroon, Denmark, Poland, Serbia, and Wales — all 2022 participants —
  did not qualify for 2026.
- Qatar qualified for the first time on sporting merit, having appeared
  only as automatic host in 2022.
- DR Congo and Haiti qualified for only the second time in their
  history, having each previously appeared solely in 1974.
- Iraq qualified for only the second time, having previously appeared
  solely in 1986.
- Austria, Norway, and Scotland returned to the World Cup after last
  qualifying in 1998.
- Türkiye (Turkey) qualified for the first time since finishing third at
  the 2002 World Cup.
- Czechia qualified for the first time since 2006.
- New Zealand, Paraguay, and South Africa returned after last appearing
  in 2010; South Africa's qualification was its first successful
  campaign since 2002.
- Algeria, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and Ivory Coast qualified for the
  first time since 2014; it was Bosnia's first successful qualification
  for any major tournament since 2014.
- Colombia, Egypt, Panama, and Sweden returned after missing 2022.
- Cape Verde, Curaçao, Jordan, and Uzbekistan qualified for a World Cup
  for the first time in their history.

## Playoff-derived teams (Pot 4 at the December 2025 draw)
Because the four UEFA playoff winners and two intercontinental playoff
winners were not yet known at the time of the December 5, 2025 Final
Draw, all six were automatically placed in Pot 4 for the draw. Their
playoffs were completed on March 26 and March 31, 2026, respectively,
shortly before the group assignments were finalized.
