# Submission Guidelines for Developers

## Who can submit

We accept submissions from solo developers and small teams (typically up to 15 people). You must have a playable build or a clear vertical slice. We do not accept concept-only pitches.

## What we look for

- **Genre:** We focus on indie-friendly genres: roguelikes, puzzle, narrative, tactics, survival, arcade. We are generally not looking for MMOs or live-service games with heavy ongoing content.
- **Scope:** Projects that can be completed in 6–18 months with a small team. We prefer a strong core loop over a huge feature list.
- **Platform:** PC (Steam, Epic, GOG) and/or console (Switch, Xbox) readiness. Mobile is considered on a case-by-case basis.

## How to submit

1. **Pitch document** – One to two pages: concept, core loop, target platform, team, timeline, and what you need from us (funding, marketing, QA, porting).
2. **Build or vertical slice** – Playable build (PC preferred for first review) or a vertical slice that shows core gameplay. Build must run without custom hardware.
3. **Send to:** submissions@pixelforgegames.com with subject line "Submission – [Game Title]". We respond within 6–8 weeks.

## Technical requirements

- Builds must run on Windows (Steam build preferred for first review).
- No placeholders that block core gameplay; art and audio can be temporary.
- If you use third-party assets or middleware, ensure you have rights and that we can ship on our target platforms.

## What happens after submission

If we are interested, we will request a full design doc, build schedule, and budget. We may offer a development deal (revenue share and/or advance) and support with QA, store pages, and marketing. Final terms are in the publishing agreement; see Revenue Share & Terms for typical splits.
