# Ashir General Hospital — Facilities & Services

## Inpatient Capacity
- **Total Licensed Beds:** 520
  - Medical/Surgical: 280
  - Labor & Delivery + Postpartum: 70
  - Pediatric Ward: 30
  - Adult ICU: 24
  - Cardiac ICU (CCU): 12
  - Pediatric ICU: 8
  - NICU (Level III): 28
  - Inpatient Rehab: 25
  - Inpatient Psychiatry: 20
  - Oncology Inpatient: 25

## Operating & Procedure Rooms
- 10 main operating theaters (including 2 hybrid ORs)
- 2 cardiac catheterization labs
- 4 endoscopy suites
- 3 interventional radiology rooms
- 6 labor & delivery rooms + 2 C-section ORs

## Emergency & Trauma
- 42-bay Emergency Department
- Level II Trauma Center
- Dedicated pediatric ER section (8 bays)
- Helipad for critical transfers
- Annual ER volume: ~95,000 visits

## Diagnostic Imaging
- 2 MRI scanners (3T and 1.5T)
- 3 CT scanners (including 256-slice)
- 1 PET-CT
- 8 ultrasound rooms
- Digital mammography and DEXA bone density
- Full-field digital X-ray across all campuses

## Laboratory
- 24/7 core lab performing ~2.2M tests/year
- Microbiology, molecular diagnostics, blood bank
- Histopathology and cytology

## Outpatient Services
- 60+ specialty clinics across Main Campus and Community Clinic
- Wellness & Preventive Care Center
- Diabetes Education Center
- Cardiac Rehabilitation Program
- Oncology Infusion Center (22 chairs)
- Hemodialysis Unit (18 stations)

## Pharmacy
- 24/7 inpatient pharmacy
- Two retail pharmacies (Main Campus + Community Clinic)
- Specialty pharmacy for oncology and chronic disease medications

## Patient Amenities
- Private and semi-private rooms
- In-room patient portal tablets for education, meal ordering, and care team messaging
- Interpreter services in 18 languages, including sign language
- On-site cafeteria, coffee shop, chapel, family lounges, and a pediatric play area
- Free Wi-Fi and on-site parking for visitors

## Community & Outreach
- Mobile health unit serving rural clinics twice weekly
- Monthly free health screenings (BP, glucose, BMI)
- Annual "Healthy Hearts" and "Diabetes Awareness" public campaigns
