# About Ashir General Hospital

Ashir General Hospital was founded in 2004 by Dr. Imran Ashir as a community-focused teaching hospital dedicated to providing world-class medical care to patients regardless of background. What began as a 40-bed clinic in the outskirts of the city has grown into a multi-specialty 520-bed tertiary care hospital serving more than 180,000 outpatient visits and 32,000 inpatient admissions every year.

The hospital operates across three campuses — the Main Campus (surgical, cardiac, and oncology services), the Women & Children's Wing, and the Ashir Community Clinic that provides affordable primary care. Ashir is an accredited teaching hospital affiliated with two regional medical universities and trains more than 120 resident doctors across 14 specialties each year.

As of 2026, Ashir General Hospital employs 62 full-time attending physicians, 210 nurses, and 180 allied health and support staff. The hospital actively manages more than 40 different disease programs spanning cardiology, oncology, neurology, endocrinology, pediatrics, obstetrics, orthopedics, gastroenterology, pulmonology, nephrology, dermatology, psychiatry, and infectious disease.

Ashir's mission is simple: **"Compassionate care, backed by evidence."** The hospital is known in the region for:

- A Level II Trauma Center with 24/7 emergency coverage
- A leading Cardiac Catheterization Lab performing 1,200+ procedures a year
- A nationally recognized Diabetes & Endocrinology program
- A Maternal-Fetal Medicine unit with a Level III NICU
- A growing Oncology Center treating both solid tumors and hematologic malignancies
