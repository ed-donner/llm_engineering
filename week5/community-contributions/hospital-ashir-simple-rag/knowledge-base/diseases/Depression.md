# Condition Summary

# Major Depressive Disorder

## Overview

Depression care at Ashir is delivered by the Department of Psychiatry & Behavioral Health and its outpatient Mood Disorders Clinic, led by Dr. Noor Al-Sayed. The program treats Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), persistent depressive disorder, bipolar depression, perinatal depression, and depression comorbid with chronic medical illness.

The clinic actively follows **1,650+ patients** with mood disorders and coordinates care with primary care, oncology, cardiology, and OB/GYN through Ashir's collaborative care model.

## Services Offered

### 1. Psychiatric Evaluation
Comprehensive 90-minute intake including PHQ-9, GAD-7, MDQ, suicide risk assessment, and functional status review. Standardized measures are repeated at every follow-up.

### 2. Pharmacotherapy
Full range of SSRIs, SNRIs, atypical antidepressants, tricyclics, augmentation strategies (lithium, atypical antipsychotics, thyroid hormone), and ketamine therapy for treatment-resistant depression.

### 3. Psychotherapy
On-site licensed therapists providing CBT, interpersonal therapy (IPT), DBT skills groups, and trauma-focused therapy. Sessions may be in-person or telehealth.

### 4. Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS)
Ashir is the regional leader in TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression, launched under Dr. Al-Sayed's leadership. Standard 6-week course with documented response rates above 55%.

### 5. Collaborative Care in Primary Care
Integrated behavioral health consultants embedded in primary care at the Community Clinic. PHQ-9-guided stepped care has improved remission rates from 31% to 54%.

### 6. Perinatal Mood Disorders Clinic
Co-run with OB/GYN (Dr. Lina Haddad) to address antenatal and postpartum depression with medication and therapy tailored for breastfeeding safety.

### 7. Crisis & Safety Services
Same-day urgent mental health appointments, 24/7 psychiatric crisis line, and seamless admission pathway to Ashir's 20-bed inpatient psychiatric unit when needed.

### 8. Integrated Care for Chronic Illness
Depression screening and treatment embedded in oncology, cardiology, and diabetes clinics.

## Outcomes (2025)

- **Response rate (50% reduction in PHQ-9) at 12 weeks:** 68%
- **Remission rate (PHQ-9 < 5) at 6 months:** 54%
- **TMS response rate (treatment-resistant depression):** 55%
- **No-show rate for initial appointments:** 9% (regional average ~20%)

## Service Tiers

- **Standard Outpatient Psychiatry:** Covered under insurance — includes psychiatric visits, medication management, and therapy.
- **Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP):** $2,800 — 4-week structured program, 9 hours/week of therapy and medication oversight.
- **TMS Course:** $7,500 — 30-session course (insurance often covers for treatment-resistant MDD).
- **Ashir Mind Wellness Membership:** $150/month — monthly therapy session, quarterly medication check, digital CBT app, and 24/7 crisis-line access.
