KENYA PENAL CODE (CAP 63) - KEY PROVISIONS AND CASE LAW

PART I: GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 7 - Territorial Application
Subject to the provisions of this Code, the criminal law of Kenya extends to every place within Kenya, including territorial waters.

Section 8 - Criminal Responsibility
A person is not criminally responsible for an act or omission that occurs independently of the exercise of his will, or for an event which occurs by accident.

Section 10 - Ignorance of the Law
Ignorance of the law does not afford any excuse for any act or omission which would otherwise constitute an offence.

Section 15 - Criminal Intent
(1) Subject to the express provisions of this Code relating to negligent acts and omissions, a person is not criminally responsible for an act or omission unless it is voluntary and either: he intends the act or omission; or he is reckless as to whether the act or omission occurs.

PART VI: OFFENCES AGAINST THE PERSON

Section 203 - Murder
Any person who of malice aforethought causes the death of another person by an unlawful act or omission is guilty of murder.

Section 204 - Punishment for Murder
Any person convicted of murder shall be sentenced to death.

Note on Death Penalty: In Muruatetu & Another v Republic [2017] eKLR, the Supreme Court of Kenya held that the mandatory death sentence for murder was unconstitutional as it violated the right to a fair trial and human dignity under Articles 25 and 28 of the Constitution. Courts now have discretion in sentencing.

Case: Francis Karioko Muruatetu & Another v Republic [2017]
Court: Supreme Court of Kenya
Principle: Mandatory death sentence for murder is unconstitutional. Judges must consider mitigating and aggravating factors in sentencing. This landmark ruling fundamentally changed criminal sentencing in Kenya.

Section 205 - Manslaughter
Any person who by an unlawful act or omission causes the death of another person is guilty of manslaughter.
Punishment: Imprisonment for life.

Section 206 - Killing on Provocation
Provocation, if sufficient, reduces murder to manslaughter. Provocation means and includes any wrongful act or insult of such a nature as to be likely, when done to an ordinary person to deprive him of the power of self-control.

Section 210 - Attempt to Murder
Any person who attempts to cause the death of another person, or does any act with intent to cause the death of another person, is guilty of a felony and is liable to imprisonment for life.

Section 251 - Assault
Any person who unlawfully assaults another is guilty of a misdemeanour.

Section 252 - Assault Causing Actual Bodily Harm
Any person who commits an assault occasioning actual bodily harm is guilty of a misdemeanour and is liable to imprisonment for five years.

PART IX: OFFENCES RELATING TO PROPERTY

Section 275 - Theft
A person who fraudulently and without claim of right takes anything capable of being stolen, or fraudulently converts to the use of any person other than the owner, is said to steal that thing.

Section 279 - Punishment for Stealing
Any person who steals anything shall, unless some other punishment is provided, be liable to imprisonment for three years.

Section 295 - Robbery
Any person who steals anything, and, at or immediately before or immediately after the time of stealing it, uses or threatens to use actual violence to any person or property in order to obtain or retain the thing stolen, is guilty of the felony termed robbery and is liable to imprisonment for fourteen years.

Section 296 - Robbery with Violence
(1) Any person who commits the felony of robbery is, if at or immediately before or immediately immediately after the time of the robbery, he wounds, beats, strikes or uses any other personal violence to any person, guilty of an offence and is liable to suffer death.
(2) If the offender is armed with any dangerous or offensive weapon or instrument, or is in company with one or more other person or persons, is liable to suffer death.

Section 304 - Handling Stolen Goods
Any person who receives anything which has been obtained by means of any act constituting a felony or misdemeanour knowing it to have been so obtained is guilty of the misdemeanour of receiving stolen property.

SEXUAL OFFENCES ACT (NO. 3 OF 2006)

Section 3 - Rape
(1) A person commits rape if: the person intentionally and unlawfully commits an act which causes penetration with his genital organs; the other person does not consent to the penetration; and the perpetrator knows that the other person does not consent.
Punishment: Imprisonment for a minimum of ten years or imprisonment for life.

Section 8 - Sexual Assault
A person who commits an act which causes penetration with a genital organ otherwise than under section 3, or causes penetration with any part of the body of a person or by use of an object, commits an act of sexual assault.
Punishment: Imprisonment for a minimum of ten years.

Section 11 - Sexual Harassment
A person in a position of authority who persistently makes any sexual advances or requests that the person knows, ought to know, are unwelcome, is guilty of sexual harassment.

NOTABLE CRIMINAL LAW CASES IN KENYA

Case: Republic v John Kamau Njoroge [2019] eKLR
Court: High Court of Kenya at Nakuru
Facts: Accused charged with murder after stabbing the deceased during a domestic dispute.
Issue: Whether the defence of provocation reduced the offence to manslaughter.
Held: The court found that while there was provocation, the accused's response was disproportionate. Convicted of murder but sentenced to 30 years following Muruatetu guidelines.

Case: Republic v Peter Maina Kariuki [2020] eKLR
Court: High Court at Nairobi
Facts: Accused charged with robbery with violence under Section 296(2) of the Penal Code.
Held: The prosecution must prove beyond reasonable doubt that the accused was armed or in company of others. Mere presence at the scene is insufficient for conviction under s.296(2).

ANTI-CORRUPTION AND ECONOMIC CRIMES ACT (NO. 3 OF 2003)

Section 39 - Corruption
A person is guilty of an offence of corruption if the person: corruptly receives, obtains, or agrees to receive or obtain a gratification for themselves or another person; or corruptly gives, confers, or agrees to give or confer a gratification to or on another person.
Punishment: Imprisonment for 10 years or a fine of KES 1,000,000 or both.

Section 45 - Duty to Report Corruption
Every public officer who has information about corruption shall report such information to the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission.

THE COMPUTER MISUSE AND CYBERCRIMES ACT, 2018

Section 3 - Unauthorized Access to Computer Systems
A person who, intentionally and without authorization, accesses the whole or any part of a computer system, commits an offence and is liable to imprisonment for 3 years or a fine of KES 5 million.

Section 22 - Cyber Harassment
A person who willfully communicates data, which is false, misleading, or fictitious, by means of a computer system, for the purposes of causing distress, psychological suffering, or bullying is guilty of an offence liable to ten years imprisonment.
