# Expert guidance for KCSE candidates choosing courses and universities

## How to use your cluster points

1. **Know your cluster**  
   Your course of interest belongs to one of the KUCCPS clusters (1, 2, 3, 4A, 4B, 5). Your weighted cluster points for that cluster determine your competitiveness for programmes in that cluster.

2. **Compare with cut-offs**  
   Each programme has a cut-off point that changes yearly. If your weighted cluster is at or above the cut-off, you have a chance at placement. If below, consider:
   - Other programmes in the same cluster with lower cut-offs
   - Related programmes in the same or another cluster
   - Diploma/certificate options that can later articulate to degree

3. **Diversify choices**  
   KUCCPS allows multiple choices. List programmes you qualify for in order of preference. Include at least one or two where your points are clearly above the typical cut-off to increase placement chances.

## Course-to-cluster mapping (quick reference)

- **Engineering, Computer Science, Architecture, Physical Sciences** → Cluster 1  
- **Medicine, Pharmacy, Nursing, Agriculture, Veterinary, Biological Sciences** → Cluster 2  
- **Commerce, Economics, Actuarial Science, Finance** → Cluster 3  
- **B.Ed Science, B.Ed Mathematics** → Cluster 4A  
- **B.Ed Arts, B.Ed Languages, Humanities teaching** → Cluster 4B  
- **Law, Journalism, Social Work, Public Administration, Arts, Social Sciences** → Cluster 5  

## Recommendations by score band (weighted cluster)

- **40+**  
  You are highly competitive. You can aim for the most competitive programmes in your cluster (e.g. Medicine, Law, Engineering at top universities). List your preferred universities and programmes first.

- **30–39**  
  Good range. You qualify for many degree programmes. Include a mix of competitive and moderate-cut-off programmes. Consider 2–3 public and 1–2 private universities if applicable.

- **20–29**  
  Moderate. Check cut-offs carefully. Include programmes with historically lower cut-offs. Consider diploma options as backup.

- **Below 20**  
  Focus on programmes with lower cut-offs, foundation/bridge programmes, or diploma/certificate routes. You can still pursue degree later through articulation or re-entry.

## At most four universities

When giving recommendations, experts typically suggest **at most four universities** that match the student’s cluster, course of interest, and cluster points. One or two can be more competitive (reach), and one or two more within or below the student’s range (safer). Always advise the student to confirm cut-offs on the KUCCPS portal.
