# Common Drug Interactions Guide

## Why Drug Interactions Matter
Drug interactions occur when one substance affects how another works. This can make medications less effective, cause unexpected side effects, or create dangerous reactions. CareFirst Pharmacy provides free drug interaction checks — bring all your medications (prescriptions, OTC, vitamins, and supplements) to the pharmacy counter any time.

## High-Risk Interaction Categories

### Blood Thinners (Warfarin, Eliquis, Xarelto)
Blood thinners are among the highest-risk medications for interactions:

| Interacting Substance | Effect | Risk Level |
|----------------------|--------|------------|
| NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen, aspirin) | Increased bleeding risk | HIGH |
| Acetaminophen (high doses) | May enhance anticoagulant effect | MODERATE |
| Vitamin K-rich foods (kale, spinach, broccoli) | Reduces warfarin effectiveness | MODERATE (warfarin only) |
| Fish oil supplements | Increased bleeding risk | MODERATE |
| Cranberry juice | May increase warfarin levels | MODERATE |
| Antibiotics (metronidazole, fluconazole, TMP-SMX) | Increased bleeding risk | HIGH |
| St. John's Wort | Decreases warfarin levels | HIGH |

**CareFirst recommendation:** Warfarin patients should maintain consistent vitamin K intake (don't eliminate greens — just eat consistent amounts). Report any new medications or supplements to your pharmacist.

### Blood Pressure Medications (ACE Inhibitors, ARBs)
| Interacting Substance | Effect | Risk Level |
|----------------------|--------|------------|
| NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) | Reduces blood pressure control, kidney risk | HIGH |
| Potassium supplements | Risk of dangerously high potassium | HIGH |
| Salt substitutes (contain potassium) | Same as above | MODERATE |
| Alcohol (excessive) | Additive blood pressure lowering, dizziness | MODERATE |
| Lithium | Increased lithium levels | HIGH |

### Statins (Atorvastatin, Simvastatin, Rosuvastatin)
| Interacting Substance | Effect | Risk Level |
|----------------------|--------|------------|
| Grapefruit juice | Increases statin levels (atorvastatin, simvastatin) | MODERATE |
| Certain antibiotics (clarithromycin, erythromycin) | Increases statin levels, muscle damage risk | HIGH |
| Gemfibrozil (fibrate) | Increased risk of rhabdomyolysis | HIGH |
| Cyclosporine | Significantly increases statin levels | HIGH |
| Colchicine | Increased myopathy risk | MODERATE |

### Diabetes Medications
| Interacting Substance | Effect | Risk Level |
|----------------------|--------|------------|
| Alcohol | Risk of hypoglycemia (with insulin, sulfonylureas) | HIGH |
| Beta-blockers | Can mask hypoglycemia symptoms | MODERATE |
| Corticosteroids (prednisone) | Raises blood sugar, counteracts diabetes meds | HIGH |
| Fluoroquinolone antibiotics | Blood sugar fluctuations | MODERATE |

### Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs)
| Interacting Substance | Effect | Risk Level |
|----------------------|--------|------------|
| Tramadol | Risk of serotonin syndrome | HIGH |
| Triptans (migraine meds) | Risk of serotonin syndrome | MODERATE |
| St. John's Wort | Risk of serotonin syndrome | HIGH |
| NSAIDs | Increased GI bleeding risk | MODERATE |
| MAOIs + tyramine-rich foods | Hypertensive crisis (MAOI users only) | CRITICAL |

## Common OTC + Prescription Interactions

### Decongestants (Pseudoephedrine, Phenylephrine)
- **Raise blood pressure** — avoid if on blood pressure medications
- **MAOIs** — dangerous hypertensive crisis (contraindicated)
- **Stimulant ADHD medications** — additive cardiovascular effects

### Antacids and PPIs (Omeprazole, Tums)
- **Certain antibiotics (fluoroquinolones, tetracyclines):** Reduced absorption — take 2 hours apart
- **Levothyroxine (thyroid medication):** Reduced absorption — take 4 hours apart
- **Iron supplements:** Reduced absorption — take 2 hours apart
- **Clopidogrel (Plavix):** Omeprazole may reduce effectiveness

### Supplement Interactions
| Supplement | Interacts With | Effect |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| St. John's Wort | Birth control pills, warfarin, SSRIs, HIV meds | Reduces effectiveness / serotonin risk |
| Calcium | Levothyroxine, certain antibiotics | Reduced drug absorption |
| Iron | Levothyroxine, antacids, certain antibiotics | Reduced absorption of both |
| Fish oil | Blood thinners | Increased bleeding risk |
| Ginkgo biloba | Blood thinners, NSAIDs | Increased bleeding risk |
| Vitamin E (high doses) | Blood thinners | Increased bleeding risk |

## Serotonin Syndrome Warning Signs
A potentially life-threatening condition from excess serotonin:
- Agitation, confusion, rapid heart rate
- High blood pressure, dilated pupils
- Muscle twitching, rigidity, tremor
- Heavy sweating, diarrhea, high fever
- **Seek emergency care immediately if suspected**

## CareFirst Pharmacist Tip
The most dangerous interactions are often between prescription drugs and things patients don't think to mention: supplements, herbal products, OTC medications, and even certain foods. Always tell your pharmacist about EVERYTHING you take — even if it seems harmless. When in doubt, ask before you take it.
