# Allergy Management Guide

## Types of Allergies

### Seasonal Allergic Rhinitis (Hay Fever)
- **Triggers:** Tree pollen (spring), grass pollen (late spring/summer), ragweed (fall), mold spores (fall)
- **Symptoms:** Sneezing, runny/stuffy nose, itchy/watery eyes, itchy throat
- **Peak seasons in our area:** April–May (tree pollen), June–July (grass), August–October (ragweed)

### Perennial Allergic Rhinitis (Year-Round)
- **Triggers:** Dust mites, pet dander (cats, dogs), cockroach droppings, indoor mold
- **Symptoms:** Same as seasonal but persistent year-round
- **Often worse indoors**, especially in winter when windows are closed

### Skin Allergies
- **Contact dermatitis:** Poison ivy, latex, nickel, fragrances, certain soaps
- **Hives (urticaria):** Can be triggered by foods, medications, insect stings, or stress
- **Eczema flares:** Often triggered by allergens, dry air, or irritants

## Pharmacist Recommendations by Symptom

### Primarily Sneezing/Runny Nose
Start with an oral antihistamine (cetirizine, loratadine, or fexofenadine)

### Primarily Nasal Congestion
Nasal corticosteroid spray (fluticasone or triamcinolone) — takes 1–2 weeks for full effect, use daily during allergy season

### Itchy/Watery Eyes
Ketotifen antihistamine eye drops (Zaditor) — can use alongside oral antihistamine

### Severe or Multiple Symptoms
Combination therapy: oral antihistamine + nasal corticosteroid spray + eye drops as needed

### Nighttime Relief / Acute Reaction
Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) for fast-acting relief, especially useful at bedtime

## When to See a Doctor
- OTC medications don't provide adequate relief after 2 weeks
- Allergies interfere with work, school, or sleep
- You develop sinus infections frequently
- You have asthma symptoms triggered by allergies
- You're interested in allergy testing or immunotherapy (allergy shots or sublingual tablets)
