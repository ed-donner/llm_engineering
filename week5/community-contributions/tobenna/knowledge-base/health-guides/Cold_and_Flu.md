# Cold and Flu Guide

## How to Tell the Difference

### Common Cold
- **Onset:** Gradual (develops over 1–3 days)
- **Fever:** Rare in adults, low-grade in children
- **Body aches:** Mild
- **Fatigue:** Mild
- **Sneezing/Runny nose:** Very common
- **Sore throat:** Common, usually the first symptom
- **Cough:** Mild to moderate, often productive
- **Duration:** 7–10 days

### Influenza (Flu)
- **Onset:** Sudden (hits fast, often within hours)
- **Fever:** Common, often 100–104°F, lasting 3–5 days
- **Body aches:** Severe
- **Fatigue:** Severe, can last 2–3 weeks
- **Sneezing/Runny nose:** Sometimes
- **Sore throat:** Sometimes
- **Cough:** Common, often dry and severe
- **Duration:** 1–2 weeks (fatigue may linger longer)

## When to See a Doctor
Seek medical attention if you experience:
- Fever above 103°F or fever lasting more than 3 days
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent chest pain or pressure
- Severe or persistent vomiting
- Confusion or dizziness
- Symptoms that improve then suddenly worsen (possible secondary infection)
- Flu symptoms in high-risk groups: adults 65+, children under 5, pregnant women, immunocompromised individuals

## Prevention Tips
- **Get your annual flu shot** — available at CareFirst Pharmacy (walk-ins welcome)
- Wash hands frequently with soap for at least 20 seconds
- Avoid touching your face
- Cover coughs and sneezes with your elbow
- Stay home when sick
- Disinfect commonly touched surfaces
- Maintain a healthy diet and adequate sleep
