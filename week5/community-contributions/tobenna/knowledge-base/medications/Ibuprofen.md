# Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)

## Overview
Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) widely used for pain relief, fever reduction, and inflammation. It is one of the most commonly purchased over-the-counter medications at CareFirst Pharmacy.

## Classification
- **Type:** NSAID (Nonsteroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug)
- **Availability:** Over-the-counter (OTC) in 200mg tablets; prescription strengths available (400mg, 600mg, 800mg)
- **CareFirst Price:** $6.99 (50-count, 200mg generic), $11.49 (brand name Advil 50-count)

## Common Uses
- Mild to moderate pain relief (headaches, toothaches, menstrual cramps, muscle aches)
- Fever reduction
- Arthritis and joint inflammation
- Minor sports injuries and sprains
- Back pain

## Dosage Guidelines
- **Adults and children 12+:** 200–400mg every 4–6 hours as needed. Do not exceed 1,200mg in 24 hours unless directed by a physician.
- **Prescription dose:** Up to 800mg per dose, 3,200mg daily maximum under medical supervision.
- **Children under 12:** Use children's ibuprofen liquid suspension. Dose by weight — consult pharmacist or pediatrician.
- **Take with food** or a full glass of water to reduce stomach irritation.

## Side Effects
### Common
- Stomach upset, nausea, heartburn
- Dizziness, mild headache
- Bloating or gas

### Serious (seek medical attention)
- Stomach bleeding (black or bloody stools, vomiting blood)
- Allergic reaction (rash, swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing)
- Chest pain, weakness on one side, slurred speech
- Kidney problems (changes in urination, swelling in ankles/feet)
- Liver problems (jaundice, dark urine)

## Drug Interactions
- **Aspirin:** May reduce aspirin's heart-protective effects. Take aspirin at least 30 minutes before or 8 hours after ibuprofen.
- **Blood thinners (warfarin, heparin):** Increased bleeding risk.
- **ACE inhibitors / ARBs:** May reduce blood pressure medication effectiveness and increase kidney risk.
- **Lithium:** Ibuprofen can increase lithium blood levels.
- **Methotrexate:** May increase methotrexate toxicity.
- **SSRIs (antidepressants):** Increased risk of GI bleeding.
- **Other NSAIDs:** Do not combine with naproxen, aspirin, or other NSAIDs.

## Contraindications
- History of allergic reaction to aspirin or other NSAIDs
- Active stomach ulcer or GI bleeding
- Severe kidney or liver disease
- Third trimester of pregnancy
- Recent or upcoming heart bypass surgery (CABG)

## Storage
- Store at room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C)
- Keep in original container, tightly closed
- Protect from moisture and light
- Keep out of reach of children

## CareFirst Pharmacist Notes
Our pharmacists recommend discussing ibuprofen use with your doctor if you take it regularly for more than 10 days. Customers over 60 or those with a history of stomach ulcers should consider acetaminophen as an alternative or use ibuprofen with a gastroprotective agent. Ask any CareFirst pharmacist for a free drug interaction check before starting ibuprofen.
