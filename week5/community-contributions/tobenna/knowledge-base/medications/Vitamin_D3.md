# Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol)

## Overview
Vitamin D3 is a fat-soluble vitamin supplement essential for calcium absorption, bone health, and immune function. It is one of the top-selling supplements at CareFirst Pharmacy, particularly during fall and winter months when sun exposure is limited.

## Classification
- **Type:** Dietary supplement / Vitamin
- **Availability:** Over-the-counter (OTC)
- **CareFirst Price:** $8.49 (120-count, 2000 IU softgels), $12.99 (5000 IU, 120-count)
- **Forms:** Softgels, tablets, liquid drops, gummies

## Common Uses
- Prevention and treatment of vitamin D deficiency
- Bone health and osteoporosis prevention
- Calcium absorption support
- Immune system support
- Mood regulation (seasonal affective disorder support)
- Muscle function

## Dosage Guidelines
- **General maintenance (adults):** 600–2,000 IU daily
- **Deficiency treatment:** 5,000–50,000 IU per week as directed by a physician based on blood levels
- **Children:** 400–1,000 IU daily depending on age
- **Infants (breastfed):** 400 IU daily (liquid drops)
- **Best taken with a meal containing fat** for optimal absorption
- **Upper limit:** 4,000 IU daily for adults without medical supervision

## Side Effects
### Common (at recommended doses)
- Generally well tolerated
- Mild nausea (rare)

### Toxicity (from excessive doses)
- Hypercalcemia: nausea, vomiting, weakness, frequent urination
- Kidney stones
- Bone pain
- Confusion, disorientation
- Heart rhythm abnormalities

## Drug Interactions
- **Thiazide diuretics:** May increase calcium levels when combined with vitamin D
- **Steroids (prednisone):** Can reduce vitamin D absorption
- **Orlistat (weight loss medication):** Reduces fat-soluble vitamin absorption
- **Cholestyramine:** Reduces vitamin D absorption
- **Calcipotriene:** Additive effects increasing calcium risk

## Who Should Take It
- Adults over 50 (decreased skin synthesis)
- People with limited sun exposure
- Those with darker skin tones (reduced vitamin D synthesis)
- Individuals with malabsorption conditions (Crohn's, celiac disease)
- Breastfed infants
- People diagnosed with vitamin D deficiency via blood test

## Storage
- Store at room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C)
- Protect from moisture and excessive heat
- Liquid drops: follow specific product storage instructions
- Keep out of reach of children
