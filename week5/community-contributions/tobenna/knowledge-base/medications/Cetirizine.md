# Cetirizine (Zyrtec)

## Overview
Cetirizine is a second-generation antihistamine used for allergy relief. It is a popular OTC choice at CareFirst Pharmacy for seasonal and year-round allergy symptoms, offering 24-hour relief with minimal drowsiness compared to first-generation antihistamines.

## Classification
- **Type:** Second-generation antihistamine (H1 receptor antagonist)
- **Availability:** Over-the-counter (OTC)
- **CareFirst Price:** $7.99 (30-count, 10mg generic), $13.49 (brand Zyrtec 30-count)
- **Forms:** Tablets, chewable tablets, liquid-filled capsules, oral syrup (for children)

## Common Uses
- Seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever): sneezing, runny nose, itchy/watery eyes
- Perennial allergic rhinitis (year-round allergies: dust mites, pet dander, mold)
- Chronic urticaria (hives)
- Itchy skin from allergic reactions

## Dosage Guidelines
- **Adults and children 6+:** 10mg once daily (or 5mg twice daily)
- **Children 2–5:** 2.5mg once daily, may increase to 5mg (once daily or 2.5mg twice daily)
- **Children 6 months–2 years:** 2.5mg once daily
- Can be taken with or without food
- May be taken morning or evening — if drowsiness occurs, take at bedtime

## Side Effects
### Common
- Drowsiness (less than first-generation antihistamines but still possible)
- Dry mouth
- Headache
- Fatigue
- Dizziness

### Uncommon
- Stomach pain, nausea
- Sore throat
- Nosebleed

### Rare/Serious
- Severe allergic reaction (paradoxical)
- Difficulty urinating
- Vision changes

## Drug Interactions
- **Alcohol:** Increased drowsiness
- **CNS depressants (benzodiazepines, opioids, sleep aids):** Additive sedation
- **Theophylline:** May slightly increase cetirizine levels (minor)
- Generally has fewer drug interactions than first-generation antihistamines

## Alternatives Available at CareFirst
- **Loratadine (Claritin):** Less drowsy but may be slightly less effective for some
- **Fexofenadine (Allegra):** Least sedating option
- **Diphenhydramine (Benadryl):** First-generation, very effective but highly sedating — best for nighttime or acute reactions
- **Nasal sprays (fluticasone):** For nasal-dominant symptoms

## Storage
- Store at room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C)
- Protect from moisture
- Syrup: check expiration, store at room temperature
- Keep out of reach of children

## CareFirst Pharmacist Notes
