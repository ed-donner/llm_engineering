# Omeprazole (Prilosec)

## Overview
Omeprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that reduces stomach acid production. It is available both over-the-counter and by prescription and is one of the best-selling medications at CareFirst Pharmacy for digestive health.

## Classification
- **Type:** Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI)
- **Availability:** OTC (20mg delayed-release) and prescription (20mg, 40mg)
- **CareFirst Price:** $9.99 (42-count OTC, 20mg generic), $14.49 (brand Prilosec OTC)
- **Forms:** Delayed-release capsules, tablets, oral suspension packets

## Common Uses
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
- Heartburn and acid indigestion (OTC use: 14-day courses)
- Stomach and duodenal ulcers
- Erosive esophagitis
- Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
- H. pylori eradication (combined with antibiotics)
- NSAID-induced ulcer prevention

## Dosage Guidelines
- **OTC heartburn:** 20mg once daily for 14 days. May repeat a 14-day course every 4 months.
- **GERD (prescription):** 20mg once daily for 4–8 weeks
- **Erosive esophagitis:** 20–40mg once daily for 4–8 weeks
- **Ulcers:** 20–40mg once daily for 4–8 weeks
- **Take 30–60 minutes before breakfast** for maximum effectiveness
- Swallow capsules whole — do not crush or chew delayed-release formulations

## Side Effects
### Common
- Headache
- Nausea, diarrhea, stomach pain
- Gas and flatulence

### Long-term use concerns (discuss with doctor)
- Vitamin B12 deficiency
- Magnesium deficiency (muscle cramps, irregular heartbeat)
- Increased fracture risk (hip, wrist, spine) with prolonged use
- C. difficile infection risk
- Possible kidney function impact

## Drug Interactions
- **Clopidogrel (Plavix):** Omeprazole may reduce clopidogrel effectiveness — consider pantoprazole instead
- **Methotrexate:** May increase methotrexate levels
- **Diazepam:** May increase diazepam levels
- **Warfarin:** May enhance anticoagulant effect
- **Iron and calcium supplements:** Reduced absorption with lower stomach acid
- **HIV medications (atazanavir, rilpivirine):** Significantly reduced absorption

## Contraindications
- Known hypersensitivity to omeprazole or other PPIs
- Concurrent use with rilpivirine-containing HIV products

## Storage
- Store at room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C)
- Keep in original packaging until use (protect from moisture)
- Keep out of reach of children

## CareFirst Pharmacist Notes
CareFirst Pharmacy recommends not using OTC omeprazole for more than 14 days without consulting a doctor. If you need acid relief frequently, our pharmacists can help assess whether a prescription-strength PPI or an alternative approach is more appropriate. Patients on long-term PPIs should discuss periodic lab work for magnesium and B12 levels with their doctor. We also carry antacids (Tums, Maalox) and H2 blockers (famotidine) for occasional heartburn that may not need a PPI.
