# Metformin (Glucophage)

## Overview
Metformin is the first-line oral medication for type 2 diabetes. It helps control blood sugar levels by decreasing glucose production in the liver and improving insulin sensitivity. CareFirst Pharmacy fills more metformin prescriptions than almost any other medication.

## Classification
- **Type:** Biguanide (oral antidiabetic)
- **Availability:** Prescription only
- **CareFirst Price:** $4.00 (30-day supply, 500mg or 1000mg generic with CareFirst Savings Card)
- **Forms:** Immediate-release tablets (500mg, 850mg, 1000mg), extended-release tablets (500mg, 750mg, 1000mg), oral solution

## Common Uses
- Type 2 diabetes mellitus (primary indication)
- Prediabetes management (off-label)
- Polycystic ovary syndrome / PCOS (off-label)
- Gestational diabetes (in some cases, under close medical supervision)

## Dosage Guidelines
- **Starting dose:** 500mg once or twice daily with meals
- **Titration:** Increase by 500mg weekly as tolerated
- **Typical maintenance:** 1,000–2,000mg daily in divided doses
- **Maximum:** 2,550mg/day (immediate-release), 2,000mg/day (extended-release)
- **Always take with food** to reduce GI side effects
- **Extended-release:** Take with evening meal; swallow whole, do not crush or chew

## Side Effects
### Common (especially during first few weeks)
- Nausea, stomach upset
- Diarrhea
- Metallic taste in mouth
- Decreased appetite
- Gas and bloating

### Serious (seek medical attention)
- **Lactic acidosis** (rare but serious): unusual muscle pain, difficulty breathing, unusual fatigue, dizziness, cold/blue skin — **seek emergency care immediately**
- Vitamin B12 deficiency (with long-term use)
- Hypoglycemia (rare when used alone, more common when combined with insulin or sulfonylureas)

## Drug Interactions
- **Alcohol:** Increases risk of lactic acidosis; limit alcohol consumption
- **Contrast dye (for CT/MRI):** Stop metformin 48 hours before and after procedures using iodinated contrast
- **Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors (topiramate, acetazolamide):** Increased lactic acidosis risk
- **Cimetidine:** May increase metformin levels
- **Diuretics, corticosteroids:** May raise blood sugar, counteracting metformin's effect

## Contraindications
- Severe kidney disease (eGFR below 30 mL/min)
- Metabolic acidosis or diabetic ketoacidosis
- Severe liver disease
- Excessive alcohol use
- Planned surgery or radiologic procedures with contrast dye

## Storage
- Store at room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C)
- Protect from light and moisture
- Keep in original container
- Keep out of reach of children
