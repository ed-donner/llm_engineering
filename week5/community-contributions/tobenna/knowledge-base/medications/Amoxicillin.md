# Amoxicillin

## Overview
Amoxicillin is a penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat a wide variety of bacterial infections. It is one of the most frequently dispensed prescription medications at CareFirst Pharmacy, especially during cold and flu season.

## Classification
- **Type:** Penicillin-class antibiotic (aminopenicillin)
- **Availability:** Prescription only
- **CareFirst Price:** $4.00 (generic, 30-count 500mg capsules with CareFirst Savings Card), $12.99 without discount
- **Forms:** Capsules (250mg, 500mg), chewable tablets, oral suspension (liquid)

## Common Uses
- Ear infections (otitis media)
- Sinus infections (sinusitis)
- Throat infections (strep throat, tonsillitis)
- Urinary tract infections
- Lower respiratory tract infections (bronchitis, pneumonia)
- Skin infections
- Dental infections and abscesses
- H. pylori infection (in combination therapy)

## Dosage Guidelines
- **Adults:** 250–500mg every 8 hours, or 500–875mg every 12 hours depending on infection severity.
- **Children:** 20–45 mg/kg/day divided into doses every 8–12 hours. Dosing based on weight and infection type.
- **Duration:** Typically 7–14 days. Complete the full course even if you feel better.
- **Can be taken with or without food.** Taking with food may reduce stomach upset.

## Side Effects
### Common
- Diarrhea
- Nausea, vomiting
- Skin rash (non-allergic)
- Headache

### Serious (seek medical attention)
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis): hives, swelling, difficulty breathing — **call 911 immediately**
- Severe watery or bloody diarrhea (possible C. difficile infection)
- Yellowing of skin or eyes (liver problems)
- Unusual bruising or bleeding
- Seizures (rare, typically with very high doses or kidney impairment)

## Drug Interactions
- **Methotrexate:** Amoxicillin may increase methotrexate levels and toxicity.
- **Warfarin:** May enhance anticoagulant effect; monitor INR.
- **Oral contraceptives:** Historically thought to reduce effectiveness, though evidence is limited. Use backup contraception as a precaution during antibiotic course.
- **Probenecid:** Increases amoxicillin blood levels (sometimes used intentionally).
- **Allopurinol:** Increased risk of skin rash when combined.

## Contraindications
- Known allergy to penicillin or cephalosporin antibiotics
- History of amoxicillin-associated liver problems
- Infectious mononucleosis (high risk of rash)

## Storage
- **Capsules/tablets:** Room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C), away from moisture
- **Liquid suspension:** Refrigerate after reconstitution. Discard after 14 days. Shake well before each dose.
- Keep out of reach of children
