# Atorvastatin (Lipitor)

## Overview
Atorvastatin is a statin medication used to lower cholesterol and reduce cardiovascular risk. It is one of the most widely prescribed medications in the United States and a top-dispensed prescription at CareFirst Pharmacy.

## Classification
- **Type:** HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor (statin)
- **Availability:** Prescription only
- **CareFirst Price:** $4.00 (30-count generic with CareFirst Savings Card)
- **Forms:** Tablets (10mg, 20mg, 40mg, 80mg)

## Common Uses
- High cholesterol (hyperlipidemia) — lowers LDL ("bad") cholesterol
- Mixed dyslipidemia — lowers triglycerides, raises HDL ("good") cholesterol
- Cardiovascular risk reduction in patients with diabetes, heart disease risk factors
- Secondary prevention after heart attack or stroke

## Dosage Guidelines
- **Starting dose:** 10–20mg once daily (40mg for patients needing >45% LDL reduction)
- **Dose range:** 10–80mg once daily
- **Can be taken at any time of day** (unlike some older statins that must be taken at bedtime)
- Can be taken with or without food
- Dose adjustments based on lipid panel results, typically checked 4–12 weeks after starting

## Side Effects
### Common
- Muscle aches and pains (myalgia) — reported by up to 10% of patients
- Headache
- Nausea, diarrhea
- Joint pain
- Nasal congestion

### Serious (seek medical attention)
- **Rhabdomyolysis** (severe muscle breakdown): unexplained severe muscle pain, weakness, dark-colored urine — **seek emergency care**
- Liver damage: yellowing of skin/eyes, dark urine, persistent nausea
- Memory problems or confusion (rare, reversible)
- New-onset diabetes (small increased risk)

## Drug Interactions
- **Grapefruit juice:** Large quantities may increase atorvastatin levels and side effects. Limit intake.
- **Cyclosporine:** Significantly increases statin levels
- **Clarithromycin / erythromycin:** Increase statin levels
- **Gemfibrozil:** Increased risk of rhabdomyolysis
- **Warfarin:** May enhance anticoagulant effect
- **Colchicine:** Increased myopathy risk
- **HIV protease inhibitors:** May increase statin levels

## Contraindications
- Active liver disease or unexplained persistent liver enzyme elevations
- Pregnancy and breastfeeding (**Category X** — can cause fetal harm)
- Known hypersensitivity to atorvastatin

## Storage
- Store at room temperature (68–77°F / 20–25°C)
- Protect from light and moisture
- Keep out of reach of children

## CareFirst Pharmacist Notes
CareFirst Pharmacy recommends taking atorvastatin consistently at the same time each day for best results. If you experience muscle pain, report it to your pharmacist — we can coordinate with your doctor about dose adjustment or trying a different statin. Patients should have liver function and lipid panels checked regularly. Women of childbearing age must use reliable contraception. CareFirst offers cholesterol screening as part of our Health Screenings service — no appointment needed on walk-in screening days (first Saturday of each month).
