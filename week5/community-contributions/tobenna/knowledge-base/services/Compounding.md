# Compounding Pharmacy Services

## What Is Compounding?
Compounding is the practice of creating customized medications tailored to an individual patient's needs. CareFirst Pharmacy's compounding lab prepares medications that are not commercially available or need to be modified for a specific patient.

## When Is Compounding Needed?
- **Allergies or sensitivities:** Patient is allergic to an ingredient in the commercial product (dyes, lactose, gluten, preservatives)
- **Dosage customization:** Pediatric doses not commercially available; geriatric patients needing lower strengths
- **Flavor modification:** Making liquid medications palatable for children (bubble gum, grape, strawberry, chocolate flavors)
- **Alternative dosage forms:** Converting a pill into a liquid, cream, suppository, or transdermal gel for patients who can't swallow pills
- **Discontinued medications:** Recreating medications that a manufacturer has stopped producing
- **Veterinary needs:** Pet medications in appropriate dosages and flavors (CareFirst compounds for cats, dogs, and other pets)

### Other Forms

## How to Get a Compounded Medication
1. Your doctor writes a prescription specifying the compounded medication needed
2. Bring or have the prescription sent to CareFirst Pharmacy
3. Our compounding pharmacist will verify the formulation and contact the prescriber if needed
4. Typical preparation time: 24–48 hours (complex formulations may take longer)
5. We'll call or text you when your compounded medication is ready

## Pricing
- Compounded medications are priced based on ingredients, complexity, and time required
- **Insurance:** Some compounded medications are covered by insurance; CareFirst will verify coverage and inform you of any out-of-pocket cost before preparing
- **Cash pricing:** Estimates provided before preparation. Typical range: $20–$120 depending on formulation
- **No charge** if we cannot fulfill the prescription to our quality standards

## Quality Assurance
- All ingredients sourced from FDA-registered suppliers
- Each batch undergoes potency verification
- Beyond-use dates (expiration) assigned based on USP guidelines
- Compounding log maintained for every preparation
