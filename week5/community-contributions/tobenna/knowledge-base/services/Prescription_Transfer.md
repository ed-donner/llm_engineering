# Prescription Transfer Service

## Overview
Switching to CareFirst Pharmacy is easy. We handle the entire transfer process for you — just provide us with your information and we'll take care of the rest.

## How to Transfer Your Prescription

### Option 1: In-Store
1. Visit CareFirst Pharmacy with your prescription bottle(s) from your current pharmacy
2. Our pharmacist will call your previous pharmacy and request the transfer
3. Most transfers are completed within 1–2 hours
4. We'll text or call you when your medication is ready

### Option 2: By Phone
1. Call CareFirst at (555) 234-5678
2. Provide the following information:
   - Your name, date of birth, and phone number
   - The name and phone number of your current pharmacy
   - The prescription number(s) you want to transfer (found on your label)
   - Your insurance information
3. Our pharmacist will handle the transfer and contact you when ready

### Option 3: Online / App
1. Log in to your CareFirst account at www.carefirstpharmacy.com or the CareFirst app
2. Select "Transfer Prescriptions"
3. Enter the requested information or upload a photo of your prescription label
4. We'll process the transfer and notify you when ready

## What You'll Need
- Name of your current pharmacy (and location/phone number)
- Prescription number(s) from your current prescription label
- Your current insurance card (we'll set up your new profile)
- Your medication allergy and health condition information

## What Can Be Transferred?
- Most prescription medications with remaining refills
- Multiple prescriptions from different pharmacies can all be transferred to CareFirst

## What Cannot Be Transferred?
- **Schedule II controlled substances** (oxycodone, Adderall, etc.) — these require a new prescription from your doctor. We can contact your prescriber to request a new prescription sent to CareFirst.
- **Expired prescriptions** (more than 1 year from date written)
- **Prescriptions with no remaining refills** — we will contact your doctor for a new prescription on your behalf
- **Compounded medication prescriptions** — the original prescription can be transferred, but prior compound formulations cannot

## Timing
- **Standard transfer:** Ready within 1–2 hours during business hours
- **After hours requests:** Processed first thing the next business day
- **Out-of-state transfers:** Allowed in most cases, may take slightly longer (2–4 hours)

## Transfer Incentive
New to CareFirst? Transfer 3 or more prescriptions and receive:
- **$25 CareFirst gift card** (applied to your next purchase)
- **Free 1-month CareFirst Plus trial membership** (includes free delivery and OTC discounts)
- One-on-one medication review with a CareFirst pharmacist

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will my current pharmacy know I'm transferring?**
A: Yes, we are required to contact them to transfer the prescription. This is a routine process.

**Q: Can I transfer back if I change my mind?**
A: Yes, prescriptions can be transferred to any pharmacy at any time.

**Q: Do I need to contact my doctor?**
A: In most cases, no. The transfer happens between pharmacies. However, if your prescription has expired or has no refills, we'll contact your doctor for a renewal.

**Q: How long until my transferred prescriptions appear in the CareFirst app?**
A: Transferred prescriptions typically appear within 2–4 hours after processing.
