# Medication Therapy Management (MTM)

## What Is MTM?
Medication Therapy Management is a comprehensive service provided by CareFirst Pharmacy's clinical pharmacists to optimize your medication regimen. MTM ensures your medications are working effectively, minimizes side effects, and eliminates unnecessary costs.

## Who Benefits from MTM?
- Patients taking 5 or more chronic medications
- Patients with multiple chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, etc.)
- Patients recently discharged from the hospital
- Patients experiencing medication side effects
- Patients who have questions about why they take each medication
- Medicare Part D beneficiaries who qualify for the CMS MTM program

## What Happens During an MTM Session?

### Comprehensive Medication Review (CMR)
A 30–45 minute one-on-one session with a CareFirst clinical pharmacist:

1. **Medication list review:** We go through every medication you take — prescriptions, OTC drugs, vitamins, and supplements
2. **Problem identification:** We check for:
   - Drug-drug interactions
   - Duplicate therapies (two drugs doing the same thing)
   - Medications no longer needed
   - Doses that may need adjustment
   - Side effects you're experiencing
   - Medications you should be taking but aren't
3. **Action plan:** We create a Personal Medication Record (PMR) and Medication Action Plan (MAP) for you
4. **Doctor coordination:** With your permission, we contact your prescriber(s) with recommendations
5. **Follow-up:** We schedule a follow-up visit to track progress

### Targeted Medication Review (TMR)
A shorter, focused review addressing a specific medication issue:
- New medication counseling
- Side effect management
- Adherence support for a specific drug
- Typically 10–15 minutes

## Services Included in MTM
- Comprehensive medication review
- Drug interaction screening
- Adherence assessment and support (pill organizers, blister packaging, medication synchronization)
- Immunization gap identification
- Chronic disease management counseling (diabetes, hypertension, asthma)
- Cost optimization (generic alternatives, patient assistance programs, formulary alternatives)
- Coordination with your healthcare team

## Cost and Insurance
- **Medicare Part D:** MTM is covered at no cost for qualifying beneficiaries (criteria set by your plan, typically based on number of medications and chronic conditions)
- **Commercial insurance:** Many plans cover MTM services; CareFirst will verify your eligibility
- **Self-pay:** $49 for a comprehensive medication review, $25 for a targeted review
- **CareFirst Plus members:** One free comprehensive review per year included
