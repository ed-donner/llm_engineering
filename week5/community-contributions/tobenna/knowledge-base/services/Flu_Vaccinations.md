# Flu Vaccinations and Immunization Services

## Overview
CareFirst Pharmacy offers a comprehensive immunization program administered by our certified immunizing pharmacists. No appointment is needed for most vaccinations — walk-ins are welcome during pharmacy hours.

## Available Vaccines

### Influenza (Flu)
- **Standard flu shot:** Quadrivalent influenza vaccine (covers 4 strains)
- **High-dose flu shot:** For adults 65+ (Fluzone High-Dose Quadrivalent)
- **Egg-free option:** Flublok recombinant vaccine for patients with egg allergies
- **Nasal spray (FluMist):** Available for healthy individuals ages 2–49
- **Season:** Available annually from September through March (while supplies last)
- **Cost:** Free with most insurance plans. $39.99 cash price without insurance.

### COVID-19
- Updated COVID-19 boosters available as recommended by CDC
- Pfizer and Moderna options
- Available for ages 6 months+ (age-appropriate formulations)
- Free for all patients regardless of insurance status

### Other Vaccines Available
- **Shingles (Shingrix):** Ages 50+, two-dose series. Covered by most insurance.
- **Pneumonia (Prevnar 20, Pneumovax 23):** Recommended for 65+ and high-risk adults
- **Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis):** Every 10 years for adults, or during pregnancy
- **Hepatitis A and B:** Two or three-dose series depending on vaccine
- **HPV (Gardasil 9):** Ages 9–45
- **Meningitis (Menactra, Menveo):** Adolescents, college students, high-risk adults
- **RSV:** For adults 60+ and pregnant individuals (seasonal)

## Who Can Get Vaccinated at CareFirst?
- **Adults:** All vaccines listed above
- **Children ages 3+:** Flu shots and select vaccines (varies by state regulation)
- **Children under 3:** Referred to pediatrician for most vaccines; COVID vaccine available at CareFirst for ages 6 months+

## The Process
1. Walk in during pharmacy hours or schedule online at www.carefirstpharmacy.com/vaccines
2. Complete a brief screening questionnaire (available on tablet in-store or online beforehand)
3. Our certified pharmacist will review your history and administer the vaccine
4. Wait 15 minutes in our observation area after vaccination
5. Receive a vaccination record card and digital record in the CareFirst app

## Insurance and Cost
- **Most commercial insurance:** Flu and routine vaccines covered at $0 co-pay under preventive care
- **Medicare Part B:** Flu and COVID vaccines covered at no cost
- **Medicare Part D:** Shingles, Tdap, and other vaccines covered (co-pay may apply)
- **Uninsured:** CareFirst offers vaccines at competitive cash prices. Ask about our Vaccine Price Match Guarantee.
- **Vaccines for Children (VFC) program:** CareFirst participates for eligible uninsured children

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need an appointment?**
A: No, walk-ins are welcome for all vaccines during pharmacy hours. However, scheduling online guarantees no wait time.

**Q: Can I get a flu shot and COVID booster at the same time?**
A: Yes, current CDC guidance allows co-administration of flu and COVID vaccines. Each shot will be given in a different arm.

**Q: What if I feel sick after my vaccination?**
A: Mild side effects (sore arm, low-grade fever, fatigue) are normal and typically resolve in 1–2 days. OTC acetaminophen or ibuprofen can help. Contact your doctor or call CareFirst at (555) 234-5678 if you have concerns.

**Q: Can I get vaccinated if I'm pregnant?**
A: Flu and Tdap vaccines are recommended during pregnancy. COVID vaccines are also approved. Discuss specific vaccines with your OB-GYN.
