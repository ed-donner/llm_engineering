# Controlled Substances Policy

## Overview
CareFirst Pharmacy follows all federal (DEA) and state regulations regarding the dispensing of controlled substances. These policies exist to prevent misuse, diversion, and to ensure patient safety.

## DEA Scheduling at CareFirst

### Schedule II (Highest restriction for dispensed medications)
**Examples:** Oxycodone, hydrocodone, morphine, fentanyl, amphetamine (Adderall), methylphenidate (Ritalin)
- **No refills permitted.** A new prescription is required each time.
- Written prescription or electronic prescription (EPCS) required — no phone-in prescriptions except in emergencies
- Maximum 30-day supply per prescription
- Must be filled within 90 days of the date written (state-dependent)
- Early fills generally not permitted — CareFirst follows a strict "not before" policy based on the days' supply

### Schedule III–IV
**Examples:** Acetaminophen with codeine (Tylenol #3), tramadol, benzodiazepines (alprazolam, lorazepam), zolpidem (Ambien)
- Up to 5 refills within 6 months from the date of the original prescription
- Phone-in prescriptions permitted from the prescriber's office
- Early refill restrictions still apply per insurance and pharmacy protocol

### Schedule V (Lowest restriction)
**Examples:** Cough preparations with small amounts of codeine (e.g., Robitussin AC), pregabalin (Lyrica)
- Similar to Schedule III–IV refill policies
- Some may be available without a prescription in certain states (not in our state)

## Identification Requirements
- **Valid government-issued photo ID** required for all controlled substance pickups
- Acceptable IDs: driver's license, state ID, passport, military ID
- If picking up for someone else, the authorized person must also present their own valid photo ID and the patient's date of birth
- ID information is logged electronically as required by law

## Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP)
- CareFirst pharmacists check the state PDMP database before dispensing controlled substances
- This helps identify potential misuse, doctor shopping, or dangerous drug combinations
- PDMP checks are a legal requirement in our state and are conducted for patient safety

## Early Refill Policy
- Controlled substance prescriptions are generally filled no earlier than 2 days before the supply runs out
- Insurance may impose stricter early-fill limits
- Exceptions may be made for documented travel, natural disasters, or when authorized by the prescriber
- Lost or stolen medication: a police report may be required before a replacement prescription can be filled

## Partial Fills
- Schedule II: Partial fills are allowed within 72 hours if full quantity is unavailable. The remaining balance must be filled within the 72-hour window.
- CareFirst will work to source the remaining medication and notify you when it's ready

## Disposal of Unused Controlled Substances
CareFirst Pharmacy participates in the DEA drug take-back program:
- A secure medication disposal kiosk is available in our store during business hours
- Drop off unused or expired controlled substances at no charge
- DEA National Take-Back Days are held twice per year — CareFirst participates each time
- Do NOT flush controlled substances unless the FDA specifically says it's safe to do so for that medication

## Pharmacist's Right to Refuse
CareFirst pharmacists have the legal right and professional responsibility to refuse to fill a controlled substance prescription if:
- There is reason to believe it was not issued for a legitimate medical purpose
- The prescription appears altered or forged
- There are concerning patterns identified through the PDMP
- It would be unsafe due to drug interactions or clinical concerns
