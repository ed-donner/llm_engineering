# Prescription Refill Policy

## Overview
CareFirst Pharmacy makes refilling your prescriptions easy and convenient. We offer multiple ways to request refills and proactive reminders to help you stay on track with your medications.

## Refill Methods

### 1. Online Refill
- Visit **www.carefirstpharmacy.com/refills**
- Log in or create an account with your prescription number
- Select the prescriptions you need refilled
- Choose pickup time or delivery option
- You'll receive a text/email notification when ready

### 2. CareFirst Mobile App
- Download the CareFirst Pharmacy app (iOS and Android)
- Scan your prescription label barcode to request a refill
- Track refill status in real time
- Set up automatic refill reminders

### 3. Phone Refill
- Call (555) 234-5678
- Press 2 for the automated refill line
- Enter your prescription number (found on your label, starts with "CF-")
- Or press 0 to speak with a pharmacy technician

### 4. In-Store Refill
- Bring your prescription bottle to the pharmacy counter
- Drop off or request a refill on the spot
- Typical wait time: 15–30 minutes (may vary during peak hours)

### 5. Text Refill
- Text your prescription number to (555) 234-5679
- You'll receive a confirmation text within minutes

## Refill Timing
- **Standard refill:** Ready within 1–2 hours of request during business hours
- **Early refill:** Most insurance plans allow refills when 75–80% of the supply has been used (typically 7 days before running out for a 30-day supply)
- **Controlled substances (Schedule II):** New prescription required each time — no refills allowed by federal law. Schedule III–V medications: up to 5 refills within 6 months of the original prescription date.
- **Expired prescriptions:** Prescriptions for non-controlled medications are valid for 1 year from the date written. After expiration, a new prescription from your doctor is required. CareFirst will fax a refill request to your doctor on your behalf.

## Automatic Refill Program (CareFirst AutoFill)
- Enroll at the pharmacy counter or through the app
- We automatically prepare your refill when it's due
- Text and email notifications sent when ready
- Opt out at any time with no penalties
- Ideal for maintenance medications (blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes)

## Refill Too Soon?
If your insurance rejects a refill as "too soon," our pharmacists can:
- Check your refill eligibility date
- Contact your insurance for a vacation/travel override (if applicable)
- Offer a partial fill or cash-price option in urgent situations
- Set up a reminder to auto-refill on the eligible date

## Prescription Transfers
If your prescription is at another pharmacy, CareFirst can transfer it for you. See our Prescription Transfer service page for details.
