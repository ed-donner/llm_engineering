# Paris, France

## Overview
Paris, the City of Light, is the capital of France and one of the most visited cities in the world. Known for its art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture, Paris is a city that captivates visitors with its elegant boulevards, world-renowned museums, and romantic atmosphere. The city is divided into 20 arrondissements (districts), each with its own distinct character.

## Best Time to Visit
The best time to visit Paris is from April to June and September to October, when the weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable. Summer (July-August) is peak tourist season with warm weather but many locals leave for vacation. Winter (November-February) is cold and grey but offers lower prices, holiday markets, and fewer tourists.

## Top Attractions
- **Eiffel Tower**: The iconic iron lattice tower offers breathtaking views from three levels. Book tickets online to skip the queue. The tower sparkles with lights every hour after dark.
- **Louvre Museum**: Home to the Mona Lisa and over 35,000 artworks. Allow at least half a day. Visit on Wednesday or Friday evenings for smaller crowds.
- **Notre-Dame Cathedral**: The famous Gothic cathedral on Ile de la Cite. Following the 2019 fire, restoration has been ongoing with plans to reopen.
- **Musee d'Orsay**: Housed in a former railway station, featuring the world's finest collection of Impressionist art including works by Monet, Renoir, and Van Gogh.
- **Montmartre & Sacre-Coeur**: The hilltop neighborhood of artists, with the white-domed basilica offering panoramic city views. Explore the charming cobblestone streets and Place du Tertre.
- **Palace of Versailles**: The opulent royal palace just outside Paris, with its Hall of Mirrors and stunning gardens. Take the RER C train from central Paris.
- **Champs-Elysees & Arc de Triomphe**: The famous avenue for shopping and strolling, ending at the triumphal arch with rooftop views.

## Food & Dining
Paris is a food lover's paradise. Essential culinary experiences include:
- **Croissants & Pastries**: Start your day at a boulangerie. Try Du Pain et des Idees or Cedric Grolet for exceptional pastries.
- **Bistro Classics**: Steak frites, duck confit, coq au vin, and French onion soup at traditional bistros like Le Bouillon Chartier.
- **Cheese & Wine**: Visit a fromagerie for artisanal cheeses. Pair with wine at a cave a vin (wine bar).
- **Crepes**: Sweet and savory crepes from street vendors in Montmartre or the Latin Quarter.
- **Markets**: Browse Marche d'Aligre or Rue Mouffetard for fresh produce, cheeses, and prepared foods.

## Getting Around
The Paris Metro is efficient, affordable, and covers the entire city. Buy a carnet of 10 tickets or use the Navigo card for unlimited weekly travel. The RER trains connect to suburbs and airports. Velib bike-sharing is excellent for exploring. Walking is the best way to discover hidden gems in each arrondissement. Uber and taxis are available but traffic can be heavy.

## Budget Tips
- Get the Paris Museum Pass for skip-the-line access to 60+ museums and monuments.
- Many museums are free on the first Sunday of each month.
- Picnic in parks with baguettes, cheese, and wine from local shops instead of dining out for every meal.
- Stay in the 10th, 11th, or 18th arrondissements for more affordable accommodation.
- Take advantage of happy hour deals (typically 5-8 PM) at bars and cafes.

## Cultural Etiquette
- Always greet shopkeepers with "Bonjour" when entering a store.
- Learn basic French phrases; locals appreciate the effort even if they speak English.
- Dress neatly; Parisians tend to dress well even for casual outings.
- Tipping is not required (service is included) but rounding up or leaving a few euros is appreciated.
- Dinner is typically served from 7:30 PM onwards; arriving earlier may mean limited options.
