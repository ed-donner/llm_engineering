# Cusco, Peru

## Overview
Cusco is the historic capital of the Inca Empire and the gateway to Machu Picchu, one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Located in southeastern Peru at an altitude of 3,400 meters (11,150 feet), this UNESCO World Heritage city blends Inca ruins with Spanish colonial architecture. Cusco's cobblestone streets, vibrant markets, and rich cultural heritage make it one of South America's most fascinating destinations.

## Best Time to Visit
The best time to visit Cusco is during the dry season from May to October. June to August offers the driest weather but is also peak tourist season, especially for trekking to Machu Picchu. May, September, and October provide good weather with smaller crowds. The wet season (November to March) brings daily afternoon rains but lush green landscapes and fewer tourists. The Inti Raymi festival in June is a spectacular celebration of the Inca sun god.

## Top Attractions
- **Machu Picchu**: The iconic Inca citadel perched high in the Andes. Book permits well in advance (at least 2-3 months). Take the train from Ollantaytambo or trek the Inca Trail (4 days) or Salkantay Trek (5 days).
- **Sacred Valley**: The fertile valley between Cusco and Machu Picchu, home to Inca ruins at Ollantaytambo, Pisac, and Moray, plus the Maras salt mines.
- **Plaza de Armas**: Cusco's main square surrounded by colonial arcades, the Cathedral of Santo Domingo, and the Jesuit Church of La Compania.
- **Sacsayhuaman**: Massive Inca fortress overlooking Cusco with enormous stone walls fitted together without mortar. Each stone weighs up to 200 tons.
- **San Pedro Market**: The bustling local market where you can try fresh juices, empanadas, and traditional Peruvian dishes at incredibly low prices.
- **Rainbow Mountain (Vinicunca)**: A striking multicolored mountain at 5,200 meters, reachable via a challenging day hike from Cusco.
- **Qorikancha (Temple of the Sun)**: The most important Inca temple, now partially covered by the Santo Domingo convent, showcasing the contrast between Inca and Spanish architecture.

## Food & Dining
Cusco offers incredible Peruvian cuisine:
- **Ceviche**: Fresh raw fish cured in citrus juices with onions, chili, and cilantro. Though coastal in origin, Cusco's restaurants serve excellent versions.
- **Lomo Saltado**: Stir-fried beef with onions, tomatoes, and fries, reflecting Chinese-Peruvian fusion cuisine.
- **Cuy (Guinea Pig)**: A traditional Andean delicacy, often roasted whole. Try it at a local picanteria for an authentic experience.
- **Alpaca**: Lean, tender meat served as steaks, burgers, or in stews. Available at most restaurants.
- **Pisco Sour**: Peru's national cocktail made from pisco (grape brandy), lime juice, egg white, and bitters.
- **Coca Tea**: Essential for altitude acclimatization. Freely available at hotels and restaurants throughout Cusco.

## Getting Around
Cusco's historic center is compact and walkable, though the altitude makes hills challenging. Taxis are inexpensive; always agree on a fare before getting in. Colectivos (shared minibuses) connect to nearby towns in the Sacred Valley. For Machu Picchu, take a bus or taxi to Ollantaytambo, then the PeruRail or Inca Rail train to Aguas Calientes. Tour operators offer group and private transport to major attractions.

## Altitude Sickness Prevention
At 3,400 meters, altitude sickness (soroche) is a real concern:
- Spend 2-3 days acclimatizing in Cusco before strenuous activities.
- Drink plenty of water and coca tea (mate de coca).
- Avoid alcohol and heavy meals on your first day.
- Walk slowly and take breaks when climbing hills.
- Consider Diamox (acetazolamide) medication; consult your doctor before the trip.
- Fly into Lima first and take the scenic train if possible.

## Budget Tips
- Cusco is very affordable by international standards. Budget travelers can eat well for under $10 per day.
- Buy the Boleto Turistico (Tourist Ticket) for access to 16 archaeological sites and museums.
- Book treks through local agencies in Cusco rather than online for better prices.
- Eat at the San Pedro Market for cheap, authentic meals.
- Travel in shoulder season (May or September-October) for lower prices and fewer crowds.
