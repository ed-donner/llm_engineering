# Tokyo, Japan

## Overview
Tokyo is Japan's bustling capital and one of the world's most exciting cities. It blends ultramodern skyscrapers with historic temples, world-class cuisine with street food, and cutting-edge technology with centuries-old traditions. With a population of over 13 million, Tokyo is a megacity that somehow manages to feel both overwhelming and intimate.

## Best Time to Visit
The best time to visit Tokyo is during spring (March to May) when cherry blossoms bloom, or autumn (October to November) when the foliage turns spectacular shades of red and gold. Summers (June to August) are hot and humid with a rainy season in June-July. Winters (December to February) are cold but clear, with fewer tourists.

## Top Attractions
- **Senso-ji Temple**: Tokyo's oldest temple in the Asakusa district, featuring the iconic Kaminarimon gate and Nakamise shopping street.
- **Shibuya Crossing**: The world's busiest pedestrian crossing, best viewed from the Starbucks above or Shibuya Sky observation deck.
- **Meiji Shrine**: A serene Shinto shrine set in a forested park near Harajuku, dedicated to Emperor Meiji.
- **Tsukiji Outer Market**: While the inner wholesale market moved to Toyosu, the outer market still offers incredible fresh sushi and street food.
- **Akihabara**: The electric town for anime, manga, electronics, and gaming culture.
- **Shinjuku Gyoen**: One of Tokyo's largest and most beautiful parks, perfect for cherry blossom viewing.
- **Tokyo Skytree**: At 634 meters, it offers panoramic views of the entire city and on clear days, Mount Fuji.

## Food & Dining
Tokyo has more Michelin stars than any other city in the world. Must-try foods include:
- **Ramen**: Visit Ichiran or Fuunji for exceptional bowls. Each neighborhood has its own style.
- **Sushi**: Splurge at a traditional sushi counter in Ginza, or enjoy affordable conveyor belt sushi (kaiten-zushi).
- **Tempura**: Light, crispy battered seafood and vegetables. Tendon Tenya offers great value.
- **Izakaya**: Japanese gastropubs serving small plates with beer and sake. Try Hoppy Street in Asakusa.
- **Convenience Store Food**: 7-Eleven and Lawson offer surprisingly excellent onigiri, sandwiches, and bento boxes.

## Getting Around
Tokyo's public transportation is among the best in the world. Get a Suica or Pasmo IC card for seamless travel on trains and buses. The JR Yamanote Line loops around central Tokyo, connecting major districts. The Tokyo Metro and Toei Subway cover the rest. Taxis are clean and safe but expensive. Walking is often the best way to explore individual neighborhoods.

## Budget Tips
- Stay in capsule hotels or hostels in Asakusa or Ueno for budget accommodation.
- Eat at standing sushi bars, ramen shops, and convenience stores for affordable meals.
- Visit free attractions like Meiji Shrine, the Imperial Palace East Gardens, and Yoyogi Park.
- Purchase a 72-hour Tokyo Metro pass for unlimited subway travel.
- Shop at 100-yen stores (Daiso) for souvenirs and essentials.

## Cultural Etiquette
- Remove shoes before entering homes, temples, and some restaurants.
- Bow when greeting people; the deeper the bow, the more respect shown.
- Do not tip; it can be considered rude.
- Be quiet on public transportation.
- Do not eat while walking; find a bench or designated eating area.
