# Southeast Asia 2-Week Itinerary

## Overview
This itinerary covers three incredible countries in two weeks: Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. It balances culture, adventure, food, and relaxation while keeping travel times manageable. Budget approximately $50-80 USD per day including accommodation, food, transport, and activities. Best visited during the dry season: November to March.

## Days 1-3: Bangkok, Thailand
**Day 1**: Arrive in Bangkok. Check into accommodation in the Khao San Road or Silom area. Explore the street food scene on Yaowarat Road (Chinatown). Try pad thai, mango sticky rice, and fresh coconut water.

**Day 2**: Visit the Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) in the morning. Cross the river to Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn) for stunning views. Afternoon at Wat Pho to see the giant reclining Buddha and get a traditional Thai massage. Evening at Asiatique night market along the river.

**Day 3**: Morning visit to Chatuchak Weekend Market (if timing works) or explore the Jim Thompson House. Afternoon at one of Bangkok's famous rooftop bars. Take an overnight train or budget flight to Chiang Mai.

## Days 4-5: Chiang Mai, Thailand
**Day 4**: Explore the Old City's temples: Wat Chedi Luang, Wat Phra Singh, and Wat Chiang Man. Afternoon visit to an ethical elephant sanctuary (Elephant Nature Park is highly recommended). Evening at the Sunday Walking Street market if timing aligns.

**Day 5**: Morning hike to Doi Suthep temple for panoramic views. Afternoon Thai cooking class to learn classics like green curry, som tum, and tom yum. Evening free for exploring the Night Bazaar or enjoying Chiang Mai's craft beer scene. Take an evening flight to Hanoi, Vietnam.

## Days 6-8: Hanoi & Ha Long Bay, Vietnam
**Day 6**: Explore Hanoi's Old Quarter on foot. Visit Hoan Kiem Lake, the Temple of Literature, and Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum. Try bun cha (grilled pork noodles), pho, and egg coffee at local street stalls. Watch a traditional water puppet show in the evening.

**Day 7-8**: Two-day, one-night Ha Long Bay cruise. Kayak through limestone karsts, explore caves, swim in emerald waters, and enjoy fresh seafood on board. Watch sunrise over the bay from the top deck. Return to Hanoi in the afternoon of Day 8. Take an overnight bus or flight to Hoi An.

## Days 9-10: Hoi An, Vietnam
**Day 9**: Explore the UNESCO-listed Ancient Town: the Japanese Covered Bridge, assembly halls, and traditional houses. Visit a tailor to have custom clothing made (allow 24 hours for completion). Rent bicycles and ride to An Bang Beach in the afternoon. Evening food tour sampling cao lau, white rose dumplings, and banh mi.

**Day 10**: Morning cooking class at one of Hoi An's famous cooking schools, starting with a market visit. Pick up tailored clothing. Afternoon boat trip on the Thu Bon River or visit the Tra Que vegetable village. Release lanterns on the river at night (especially magical during the full moon lantern festival). Fly to Siem Reap, Cambodia.

## Days 11-13: Siem Reap, Cambodia
**Day 11**: Full day at Angkor Wat complex. Start with sunrise at Angkor Wat (arrive by 5 AM). Explore Angkor Thom, the Bayon temple with its enigmatic stone faces, and Ta Prohm (the "Tomb Raider" temple). Buy a 3-day pass for the best value.

**Day 12**: Continue exploring Angkor: visit the outer temples including Banteay Srei (known for intricate carvings), Pre Rup, and East Mebon. Afternoon visit to the Landmine Museum. Evening at Pub Street for dinner and nightlife.

**Day 13**: Morning visit to the floating villages on Tonle Sap Lake. Afternoon free for a traditional Khmer massage, shopping at the Old Market, or taking a Cambodian cooking class. Try amok (coconut curry steamed in banana leaves) and lok lak (stir-fried beef).

## Day 14: Departure
Morning free for last-minute shopping or relaxation. Depart from Siem Reap airport.

## Budget Breakdown (Per Person)
- Accommodation: $15-30/night (hostels to mid-range guesthouses)
- Food: $10-20/day (street food and local restaurants)
- Activities: $10-25/day (temple entry, tours, cooking classes)
- Transport: $150-300 total (budget flights and local transport)
- **Total estimated: $700-1,200 for 14 days**

## Essential Tips
- Get a local SIM card at each airport for affordable data.
- Carry small bills for tipping and markets.
- Dress modestly for temple visits (cover shoulders and knees).
- Negotiate prices at markets but be fair; a few cents matter more to vendors than to you.
- Stay hydrated; the heat and humidity can be intense.
