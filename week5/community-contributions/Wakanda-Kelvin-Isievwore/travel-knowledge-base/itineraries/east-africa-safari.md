# East Africa Safari Itinerary

## Overview
This 10-day itinerary covers the best of Kenya and Tanzania, offering world-class wildlife viewing, stunning landscapes, and authentic cultural experiences. The safari circuit includes the Masai Mara, Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, and Lake Manyara. Best visited during the Great Migration (July-October) or the calving season (January-February). Budget approximately $150-300 USD per day depending on camping vs. lodge accommodation.

## Days 1-2: Nairobi, Kenya
**Day 1**: Arrive in Nairobi. Visit the David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage (morning visit at 11 AM only, book online). Afternoon at the Giraffe Centre in Karen to hand-feed endangered Rothschild giraffes. Explore the Karen Blixen Museum (of "Out of Africa" fame). Evening dinner at Carnivore restaurant for a unique game meat experience or at Mama Oliech for authentic Kenyan cuisine including ugali, sukuma wiki, and nyama choma.

**Day 2**: Morning at Nairobi National Park, the only national park within a major city. See lions, rhinos, and giraffes against the Nairobi skyline. Afternoon at the Kazuri Beads factory and the Maasai Market for handcrafted souvenirs. Prepare for the safari: stock up on sunscreen, batteries, and snacks. Evening safari briefing with your guide.

## Days 3-5: Masai Mara National Reserve, Kenya
**Day 3**: Early morning departure from Nairobi (5-6 hour drive or 45-minute flight). Afternoon game drive in the Masai Mara. The Mara is famous for the Big Five: lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhinoceros. Check into your tented camp or lodge.

**Day 4**: Full-day game drive in the Masai Mara. During the Great Migration (July-October), witness millions of wildebeest and zebra crossing the Mara River in one of nature's most dramatic spectacles. Look for cheetahs on the open plains and hippos in the Mara River. Optional hot air balloon safari at sunrise (book in advance, approximately $450-500).

**Day 5**: Morning game drive focusing on predator activity (lions and leopards are most active in early morning). Visit a Maasai village for a cultural experience: learn about their semi-nomadic lifestyle, traditional dances, and beadwork. The Maasai are known for their distinctive customs, including their red shuka garments and jumping dance (adumu). Afternoon transfer towards the Tanzania border.

## Days 6-7: Serengeti National Park, Tanzania
**Day 6**: Cross into Tanzania at the Isebania/Sirari border. Drive through the western Serengeti corridor. The Serengeti's endless plains (the name means "endless plain" in Maasai) support the largest terrestrial animal migration in the world. Afternoon game drive spotting resident wildlife: elephants, giraffes, topis, and if lucky, a leopard in an acacia tree. Sunset over the Serengeti is unforgettable.

**Day 7**: Full-day game drive through different Serengeti ecosystems: the Seronera valley (central) for leopards and lions, the kopjes (rock formations) where lions rest and cheetahs hunt, and the river crossings if visiting during migration season. Birdwatchers will spot over 500 species including secretary birds, lilac-breasted rollers, and various raptors. Evening around the campfire with stories from your guide about African bush lore.

## Days 8-9: Ngorongoro Crater, Tanzania
**Day 8**: Morning drive from Serengeti to Ngorongoro Conservation Area. Stop at Olduvai Gorge, one of the most important paleoanthropological sites in the world where early human fossils were discovered. Arrive at the crater rim with breathtaking views of the caldera below. The Ngorongoro Crater is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's largest intact volcanic caldera, spanning 260 square kilometers.

**Day 9**: Full-day game drive inside the crater. The crater floor is home to approximately 25,000 large animals, making it one of the densest wildlife concentrations in Africa. Excellent chances of seeing the critically endangered black rhinoceros, flamingos on Lake Magadi, and large prides of lions. The crater's enclosed environment means animals don't migrate, providing consistent year-round viewing. Picnic lunch near the hippo pool. Afternoon ascend the crater and drive to Lake Manyara area.

## Day 10: Lake Manyara & Departure
Morning game drive at Lake Manyara National Park, famous for its tree-climbing lions and large flocks of flamingos along the alkaline lake. The park also features diverse habitats from groundwater forest to open grasslands, supporting elephants, baboons, and blue monkeys. Afternoon transfer to Arusha (2 hours) or Kilimanjaro International Airport for departure.

## Safari Packing List
- Neutral-colored clothing (khaki, olive, brown) for game drives
- Warm fleece or jacket for early morning drives and high-altitude areas
- Wide-brimmed hat and quality sunglasses
- Binoculars (essential, 8x42 or 10x42 recommended)
- Camera with zoom lens (200mm minimum for wildlife photography)
- Insect repellent with DEET and anti-malaria medication
- Dust mask or buff for open-vehicle drives
- Headlamp for camp at night
- Reusable water bottle (camps/lodges provide filtered water)

## Health and Safety
- Malaria prophylaxis is essential for East Africa. Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel.
- Yellow fever vaccination may be required depending on your country of origin.
- Drink only bottled or filtered water.
- Never leave the vehicle during game drives unless your guide says it's safe.
- Respect wildlife distances; never attempt to touch or feed animals.
- The sun is intense near the equator; reapply sunscreen frequently.

## Budget Breakdown (Per Person)
- **Budget Camping Safari**: $150-200/day (basic camping, cook-prepared meals, shared vehicle)
- **Mid-Range Lodge Safari**: $250-400/day (comfortable lodges, en-suite rooms, experienced guides)
- **Luxury Safari**: $500-1,500+/day (luxury tented camps, private vehicles, bush dining experiences)
- Park fees: $60-80/day (Masai Mara), $60-70/day (Serengeti), $70/day (Ngorongoro)
- Border crossing fees: ~$50 for Tanzania visa on arrival
- Hot air balloon: $450-500
- **Total estimated (mid-range): $2,500-4,000 for 10 days**

## Booking Tips
- Book through a reputable local operator for better value than international tour companies.
- Group safaris (6-8 people) are significantly cheaper than private safaris.
- The Great Migration is peak season with highest prices; shoulder months (June, November) offer good viewing with lower rates.
- Confirm what's included: park fees, meals, water, and transfers can significantly affect total cost.
- Tip your guide $15-20 per person per day and camp/lodge staff $5-10 per day.
