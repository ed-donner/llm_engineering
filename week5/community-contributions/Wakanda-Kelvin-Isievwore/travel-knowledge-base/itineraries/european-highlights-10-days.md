# European Highlights 10-Day Itinerary

## Overview
This itinerary covers four iconic European cities connected by high-speed rail: Paris, Amsterdam, Berlin, and Prague. It offers a mix of art, history, nightlife, and cuisine while using efficient train connections. Budget approximately $100-150 EUR per day. Best visited May to September for warm weather and outdoor activities, or December for magical Christmas markets.

## Days 1-3: Paris, France
**Day 1**: Arrive in Paris. Check into accommodation in the Marais or Latin Quarter. Walk along the Seine to Notre-Dame Cathedral (view exterior) and explore Ile Saint-Louis for Berthillon ice cream. Evening stroll to the Eiffel Tower for the hourly light show.

**Day 2**: Morning at the Louvre (book timed entry). Lunch at a bistro in the Tuileries area. Afternoon at the Musee d'Orsay for Impressionist masterpieces. Walk through Saint-Germain-des-Pres and stop at Cafe de Flore or Les Deux Magots. Evening in Montmartre: climb to Sacre-Coeur for sunset views, explore Place du Tertre, and dinner at a local bistro.

**Day 3**: Morning at Versailles (take RER C, arrive early to beat crowds). Explore the palace, Hall of Mirrors, and gardens. Return to Paris for afternoon shopping on Champs-Elysees or exploring Le Marais. Evening: final Parisian dinner, perhaps at Le Bouillon Chartier for classic French cuisine at affordable prices.

**Travel**: Take the Thalys/Eurostar high-speed train to Amsterdam (3.5 hours).

## Days 4-5: Amsterdam, Netherlands
**Day 4**: Explore the canal ring on foot or by bike. Visit the Anne Frank House (book well in advance). Lunch at Albert Cuyp Market. Afternoon at the Rijksmuseum to see Rembrandt's Night Watch and Vermeer's works. Evening canal cruise with wine and cheese.

**Day 5**: Morning at the Van Gogh Museum. Rent a bicycle and ride through Vondelpark. Afternoon exploring the Jordaan neighborhood's boutiques, galleries, and brown cafes (traditional Dutch pubs). Visit a local cheese shop for Gouda tasting. Evening in the De Pijp neighborhood for Indonesian rijsttafel (rice table) dinner, reflecting Amsterdam's colonial history.

**Travel**: Take the ICE/IC train to Berlin (6.5 hours, or take an overnight option).

## Days 6-8: Berlin, Germany
**Day 6**: Explore Cold War history: Berlin Wall Memorial, Checkpoint Charlie, and the East Side Gallery (longest remaining section of the wall). Walk through Brandenburg Gate. Afternoon at the Holocaust Memorial (Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe). Evening in Kreuzberg for Turkish street food (Berlin has the largest Turkish community outside Turkey) and craft beer.

**Day 7**: Museum Island day: Pergamon Museum and Neues Museum (home of the Nefertiti bust). Lunch at Markthalle Neun in Kreuzberg. Afternoon in the Tiergarten park, followed by a visit to the Reichstag building (book free rooftop visit in advance). Evening exploring Berlin's legendary nightlife scene in Friedrichshain.

**Day 8**: Day trip to Potsdam to visit Sanssouci Palace and its stunning terraced gardens (the "Versailles of Germany"). Return to Berlin for a currywurst at Curry 36 and evening at a rooftop bar overlooking the city. Alternative: explore Berlin's street art scene with a guided tour through Kreuzberg and Friedrichshain.

**Travel**: Take the EC train to Prague (4.5 hours).

## Days 9-10: Prague, Czech Republic
**Day 9**: Explore the Old Town: Astronomical Clock, Old Town Square, and the Jewish Quarter (Josefov) with its haunting synagogues and cemetery. Cross the Charles Bridge (go early morning to avoid crowds and see street musicians). Climb the tower for views. Lunch at a traditional Czech restaurant: try svickova (marinated beef with cream sauce) and trdelnik (chimney cake). Afternoon at Prague Castle complex, including St. Vitus Cathedral and the Golden Lane.

**Day 10**: Morning in the Mala Strana (Lesser Quarter) neighborhood, visiting the Lennon Wall and Petrin Hill for panoramic views. Take the funicular up and walk through the rose gardens. Afternoon beer tour: Czech Republic has the highest beer consumption per capita in the world. Visit local pubs for Pilsner Urquell, Staropramen, and craft beers at remarkably low prices ($1-3 per pint). Evening farewell dinner in a traditional Czech beer hall.

## Rail Pass Information
Consider a Eurail pass if under 27 (youth discount). Otherwise, point-to-point tickets booked in advance are often cheaper:
- Paris to Amsterdam: Thalys, from 35 EUR booked early
- Amsterdam to Berlin: ICE, from 40 EUR booked early
- Berlin to Prague: EC, from 19 EUR booked early
- Book through trainline.com or individual rail operators

## Budget Breakdown (Per Person)
- Accommodation: $40-80/night (hostels to budget hotels)
- Food: $25-40/day (mix of street food, markets, and restaurants)
- Activities: $15-30/day (museum passes, tours)
- Rail Transport: $100-200 total (booked in advance)
- **Total estimated: $1,200-2,000 for 10 days**

## Essential Tips
- The Paris Museum Pass (2 or 4 days) saves money and time at major attractions.
- Amsterdam's I Amsterdam City Card includes transport and museum entries.
- Berlin's WelcomeCard offers discounts on attractions and free public transport.
- Prague is significantly cheaper than Western European cities; save splurge dinners for here.
- Always validate train tickets before boarding in continental Europe.
- Most European cities have excellent tap water; refill bottles at public fountains.
