# Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

## Diagnostic Criteria (any one of the following)
- Fasting plasma glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL)
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL) during 75 g OGTT
- HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
- Random plasma glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L with symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, unexplained weight loss)

## Glycaemic Targets
| Patient | HbA1c Target |
|---|---|
| General (NICE) | ≤ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) on lifestyle/metformin |
| General (ADA) | < 53 mmol/mol (< 7%) |
| Elderly / frailty / high hypoglycaemia risk | ≤ 58–64 mmol/mol (7.5–8.0%) |

## First-Line Treatment
1. Metformin (if eGFR ≥ 30 mL/min/1.73m², not contraindicated)
   - Start: 500 mg once or twice daily with meals
   - Target: up to 2000 mg/day in divided doses
   - Withhold if eGFR < 30; reduce dose if eGFR 30–45

## Second-Line Agents (add-on to metformin)
- **SGLT-2 inhibitor** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin): preferred in HF or CKD
- **GLP-1 agonist** (semaglutide, liraglutide): preferred for weight loss or CV disease
- **DPP-4 inhibitor** (sitagliptin): weight-neutral, low hypoglycaemia risk
- **Sulfonylurea** (gliclazide): cheap; risk of hypoglycaemia and weight gain
- **Insulin**: required when oral agents fail; start with basal insulin (glargine/detemir)

## Monitoring
- HbA1c every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months
- eGFR and urine ACR annually
- Retinal screening annually
- Foot examination annually (10 g monofilament, vibration, pulses)
- Blood pressure target: < 130/80 mmHg

## Hypoglycaemia Management
- Mild (conscious): 15–20 g fast-acting carbohydrate (glucose tablets, fruit juice)
- Severe (unconscious): IV dextrose 10% or IM glucagon 1 mg

## Reference
ADA Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes 2024; NICE NG28 (updated 2022)
