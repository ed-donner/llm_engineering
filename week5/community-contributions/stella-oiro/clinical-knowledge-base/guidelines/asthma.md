# Asthma

## Definition
Chronic inflammatory airway disease characterised by variable airflow obstruction, bronchial hyperresponsiveness, and symptoms of wheeze, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and cough (especially nocturnal).

## Diagnosis
- Spirometry: FEV1/FVC ratio < 0.70 with ≥ 12% (and ≥ 200 mL) improvement post-bronchodilator
- Peak Flow variability > 20% over 2+ weeks (home monitoring)
- Positive bronchial provocation test (methacholine) if spirometry normal

## Severity Classification
| Step | Symptom frequency | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Intermittent (< 2 days/week) | SABA PRN |
| 2 | Mild persistent | Low-dose ICS + SABA |
| 3 | Moderate persistent | Low-dose ICS/LABA or medium ICS |
| 4 | Severe persistent | Medium/high ICS/LABA |
| 5 | Severe uncontrolled | Add tiotropium, biologics, or oral prednisolone |

**ICS** = inhaled corticosteroid (beclometasone, budesonide, fluticasone)
**LABA** = long-acting beta-2 agonist (salmeterol, formoterol)
**SABA** = short-acting beta-2 agonist (salbutamol / albuterol)

## Acute Exacerbation — Severity
| Parameter | Moderate | Severe | Life-threatening |
|---|---|---|---|
| PEFR | 50–75% best | 33–50% best | < 33% best |
| SpO2 | ≥ 92% | < 92% | < 92% + silent chest |
| Able to speak | Full sentences | Phrases | Cannot speak |

## Acute Exacerbation — Treatment
1. Oxygen: target SpO2 94–98%
2. SABA: salbutamol 2.5–5 mg nebulised (or 4–10 puffs via spacer) every 15–20 minutes
3. Ipratropium bromide 0.5 mg nebulised (add in severe/life-threatening)
4. Oral prednisolone 40–50 mg once daily for 5 days
5. IV magnesium sulfate 2 g over 20 minutes (severe/life-threatening)
6. ICU if refractory: consider IV aminophylline, intubation

## Inhaler Technique
- Shake MDI, exhale fully, seal lips around mouthpiece
- Press canister and inhale slowly over 3–5 seconds
- Hold breath for 10 seconds
- Spacer recommended for all pMDI devices
