# Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

## Definition
- Normal: systolic < 120 mmHg AND diastolic < 80 mmHg
- Elevated: systolic 120–129 AND diastolic < 80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: systolic 130–139 OR diastolic 80–89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: systolic ≥ 140 OR diastolic ≥ 90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: systolic > 180 and/or diastolic > 120 mmHg

## Diagnosis
- Confirmed with at least 2 measurements on 2 separate occasions
- Home blood pressure monitoring (HBPM) or ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) preferred to confirm white-coat effect

## First-Line Pharmacological Treatment
| Patient Group | Preferred Agent |
|---|---|
| General adult | ACE inhibitor or ARB, thiazide diuretic, or CCB |
| Black patients (any age) | Thiazide diuretic or CCB |
| Aged ≥ 55 | Thiazide diuretic or CCB |
| CKD with proteinuria | ACE inhibitor or ARB |

## Blood Pressure Targets
- General population: < 130/80 mmHg
- Age ≥ 65: < 130/80 mmHg (if tolerated)
- CKD: < 130/80 mmHg
- Diabetes: < 130/80 mmHg

## Lifestyle Modifications (ABCDE)
- **A**lcohol reduction: ≤ 2 drinks/day for men, ≤ 1 for women
- **B**MI/weight: target BMI < 25 kg/m²
- **C**essation of smoking
- **D**iet: DASH diet, reduce sodium < 2.3 g/day (ideally < 1.5 g/day)
- **E**xercise: 150 minutes moderate-intensity aerobic per week

## Hypertensive Emergency
- BP > 180/120 with end-organ damage (AKI, encephalopathy, dissection, pulmonary oedema)
- Admit ICU; IV labetalol, nicardipine, or sodium nitroprusside
- Reduce MAP by no more than 25% in first hour to avoid ischaemia

## Reference
ACC/AHA 2017 Guideline for High Blood Pressure; NICE NG136 (2019)
