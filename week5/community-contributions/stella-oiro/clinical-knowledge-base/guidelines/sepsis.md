# Sepsis and Septic Shock

## Definition (Sepsis-3 Criteria)
- **Sepsis**: Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection
  - SOFA score increase ≥ 2 points from baseline
- **Septic shock**: Sepsis + vasopressor requirement to maintain MAP ≥ 65 mmHg + serum lactate > 2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation

## Quick SOFA (qSOFA) — Bedside Screening
Score 1 point each:
- Respiratory rate ≥ 22 breaths/min
- Altered mental status (GCS < 15)
- Systolic BP ≤ 100 mmHg

qSOFA ≥ 2: consider sepsis, perform full SOFA assessment

## The Sepsis Six (to be completed within 1 hour)
1. **Give oxygen**: target SpO2 ≥ 94%
2. **Take blood cultures**: before antibiotics (do not delay antibiotics for cultures)
3. **Give IV antibiotics**: broad-spectrum within 1 hour of recognition
4. **Take serum lactate**: repeat at 2 hours if > 2 mmol/L
5. **Give IV fluids**: 30 mL/kg crystalloid if hypotensive or lactate ≥ 4 mmol/L
6. **Monitor urine output**: catheterise; target ≥ 0.5 mL/kg/hr

## Empirical Antibiotic Regimens (adjust based on source)
| Suspected Source | Regimen (typical UK/empirical) |
|---|---|
| Unknown / septicaemia | Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV TDS ± vancomycin |
| Urinary tract | Co-amoxiclav IV or ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV |
| Community-acquired pneumonia | Co-amoxiclav + clarithromycin IV |
| Intra-abdominal | Piperacillin-tazobactam or meropenem |
| Neutropenic sepsis | Tazocin + amikacin; add vancomycin if MRSA risk |

## Vasopressors
- First-line: noradrenaline (norepinephrine) via central line
- Target MAP ≥ 65 mmHg
- Add vasopressin 0.03 units/min if noradrenaline > 0.25 mcg/kg/min
- Consider hydrocortisone 200 mg/day if vasopressors escalating

## Lactate Targets
- Initial lactate ≥ 4 mmol/L: aggressive resuscitation
- Target: clearance ≥ 10% every 2 hours
- Lactate normalisation associated with improved outcomes
