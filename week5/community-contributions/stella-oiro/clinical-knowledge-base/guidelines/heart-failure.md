# Heart Failure

## Definition
Heart failure (HF) is a clinical syndrome in which the heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs, or can only do so at elevated filling pressures.

## Classification
| Type | EF | Description |
|---|---|---|
| HFrEF | < 40% | Reduced ejection fraction ("systolic HF") |
| HFmrEF | 40–49% | Mildly reduced EF |
| HFpEF | ≥ 50% | Preserved EF ("diastolic HF") |

## NYHA Functional Classification
- **Class I**: No symptoms with ordinary activity
- **Class II**: Mild symptoms; slight limitation with ordinary activity
- **Class III**: Marked limitation; comfortable at rest
- **Class IV**: Symptoms at rest; unable to carry out any activity without discomfort

## Diagnostic Investigations
- BNP > 35 pg/mL or NT-proBNP > 125 pg/mL (screening)
- Echocardiography: EF, wall motion, valve function, filling pressures
- ECG, FBC, U&E, TFTs, LFTs, ferritin, HbA1c

## HFrEF — Disease-Modifying Therapy ("Fantastic Four")
All four classes should be up-titrated to target dose:
1. **ACE inhibitor / ARB / ARNI** (sacubitril/valsartan preferred if tolerated)
2. **Beta-blocker** (bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol succinate)
3. **MRA** — mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (spironolactone or eplerenone)
4. **SGLT-2 inhibitor** (dapagliflozin or empagliflozin)

## Acute Decompensated Heart Failure
- IV diuretics: furosemide 40–80 mg IV (double if already on oral furosemide)
- Position: sit upright, high-flow oxygen if SpO2 < 94%
- Monitor urine output hourly (target 0.5–1 mL/kg/hr)
- Daily weight; fluid restrict to 1.5–2 L/day if hyponatraemic
- Monitor electrolytes daily; replace K+ and Mg2+ as needed

## Target Doses (HFrEF)
| Drug | Starting dose | Target dose |
|---|---|---|
| Ramipril | 1.25 mg BD | 5 mg BD |
| Bisoprolol | 1.25 mg OD | 10 mg OD |
| Spironolactone | 25 mg OD | 50 mg OD |
| Dapagliflozin | 10 mg OD | 10 mg OD |
