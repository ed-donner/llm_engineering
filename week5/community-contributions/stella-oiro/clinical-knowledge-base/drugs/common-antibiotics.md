# Common Antibiotics — Reference Guide

## Penicillins
| Drug | Spectrum | Typical Dose (Adult) | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amoxicillin | Gram+, some Gram- | 500 mg TDS PO | URTI, otitis media, pneumonia |
| Co-amoxiclav | Broad (+ beta-lactamase) | 625 mg TDS PO / 1.2 g TDS IV | Bite wounds, UTI, pelvic |
| Flucloxacillin | Staphylococci (MSSA) | 500 mg QDS PO / 1–2 g QDS IV | Cellulitis, osteomyelitis |
| Piperacillin-tazobactam | Very broad (incl. Pseudomonas) | 4.5 g TDS–QDS IV | Severe infections, Pseudomonas cover |

## Cephalosporins
| Drug | Generation | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cefalexin | 1st | 500 mg TDS PO | Mild skin, UTI |
| Cefuroxime | 2nd | 750 mg–1.5 g TDS IV | Surgical prophylaxis, pneumonia |
| Ceftriaxone | 3rd | 1–2 g OD IV | Meningitis, severe pneumonia, sepsis |

## Macrolides
| Drug | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Clarithromycin | 500 mg BD PO/IV | Atypical pneumonia, H. pylori (triple therapy) |
| Azithromycin | 500 mg OD x3 days PO | STI (chlamydia), CAP; good tissue penetration |

## Fluoroquinolones
| Drug | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg BD PO / 400 mg BD IV | UTI (complicated), GI infections, bone |
| Levofloxacin | 500 mg OD PO/IV | CAP, sinusitis |
*Caution: tendon rupture, QTc prolongation, avoid in epilepsy*

## Tetracyclines
| Drug | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Doxycycline | 100 mg BD PO | Chlamydia, Lyme disease, malaria prophylaxis, atypicals |

## Nitroimidazoles
| Drug | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Metronidazole | 400 mg TDS PO / 500 mg TDS IV | Anaerobes, C. difficile, surgical prophylaxis |
*Avoid alcohol; disulfiram-like reaction*

## Glycopeptides (IV only)
| Drug | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Vancomycin | 25–30 mg/kg/day IV (divided BD) | MRSA, C. difficile (PO for GI only) |
| Teicoplanin | Loading 6 mg/kg BD x3 doses, then OD | MRSA; easier monitoring than vancomycin |

## Carbapenems (IV only — reserve antibiotics)
| Drug | Typical Dose | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meropenem | 500 mg–1 g TDS IV | Severe/resistant infections, ESBL |
| Ertapenem | 1 g OD IV | Home IV therapy (no Pseudomonas cover) |

## Key Allergy Notes
- Penicillin allergy: cross-reactivity with cephalosporins < 2%; verify true allergy history
- Document exact reaction (rash vs anaphylaxis); refer for allergy testing where possible
