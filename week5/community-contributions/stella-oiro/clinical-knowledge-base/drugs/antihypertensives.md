# Antihypertensive Drug Reference

## ACE Inhibitors (ACEi)
| Drug | Dose Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ramipril | 1.25–10 mg OD | First-line; also in HF, CKD, post-MI |
| Lisinopril | 2.5–40 mg OD | Once daily; renal protective |
| Perindopril | 2–8 mg OD | Good evidence in CAD |

**Side effects**: Dry cough (10–15%), hyperkalaemia, first-dose hypotension, angioedema (rare but serious)
**Contraindications**: Bilateral renal artery stenosis, pregnancy, history of angioedema with ACEi
**Monitoring**: U&E and creatinine at baseline, 1–2 weeks after starting/dose change

## Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARB)
| Drug | Dose Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Losartan | 25–100 mg OD | Use if ACEi cough; renal protective |
| Candesartan | 4–32 mg OD | Also licensed for HF |
| Valsartan | 80–320 mg OD | Also in HF (as sacubitril/valsartan) |

**Note**: No cough with ARBs; same renal monitoring as ACEi; do NOT combine ACEi + ARB

## Calcium Channel Blockers (CCB)
| Drug | Type | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amlodipine | Dihydropyridine | 5–10 mg OD | Preferred in elderly, Black patients |
| Nifedipine LA | Dihydropyridine | 30–90 mg OD | Angina + hypertension |
| Diltiazem | Non-dihydropyridine | 120–360 mg OD (SR) | Rate control; avoid with beta-blocker |
| Verapamil | Non-dihydropyridine | 120–480 mg OD (SR) | Rate control; avoid with beta-blocker |

**Side effects (dihydropyridines)**: Peripheral oedema, flushing, headache, reflex tachycardia

## Thiazide/Thiazide-like Diuretics
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Indapamide | 1.5 mg SR OD | Preferred thiazide-like; metabolically neutral |
| Bendroflumethiazide | 2.5 mg OD | Older agent; less preferred |
| Chlortalidone | 12.5–25 mg OD | Strong CV evidence (ALLHAT trial) |

**Side effects**: Hypokalaemia, hyponatraemia, hyperuricaemia, hyperglycaemia, erectile dysfunction

## Beta-Blockers
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bisoprolol | 1.25–10 mg OD | Cardioselective; HF, rate control |
| Atenolol | 25–100 mg OD | Less preferred (poor stroke reduction) |
| Amlodipine/bisoprolol | Combination | Convenient for adherence |

**Side effects**: Fatigue, cold peripheries, bradycardia, masks hypoglycaemia
**Contraindications**: Severe asthma (relative), heart block, cardiogenic shock

## Alpha-Blockers (Add-on therapy)
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Doxazosin | 1–16 mg OD | Good for men with BPH |

## Combination Approach (NICE NG136 pathway)
- Step 1: ACEi/ARB or CCB
- Step 2: ACEi/ARB + CCB
- Step 3: ACEi/ARB + CCB + thiazide diuretic
- Step 4 (resistant): + aldosterone antagonist (spironolactone 25 mg) OR alpha-blocker OR beta-blocker

**Resistant hypertension**: BP uncontrolled on ≥ 3 agents including a diuretic; refer to specialist
