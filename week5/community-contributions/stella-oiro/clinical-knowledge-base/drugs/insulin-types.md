# Insulin Types and Regimens

## Insulin Classification
| Type | Onset | Peak | Duration | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rapid-acting analogue | 10–20 min | 1–3 h | 3–5 h | Aspart (NovoRapid), Lispro (Humalog), Glulisine (Apidra) |
| Short-acting (soluble) | 30–60 min | 2–4 h | 6–8 h | Actrapid, Humulin S |
| Intermediate-acting | 1–2 h | 4–8 h | 12–18 h | Insulatard (NPH), Humulin I |
| Long-acting analogue | 1–2 h | Flat | 20–26 h | Glargine (Lantus, Toujeo), Detemir (Levemir) |
| Ultra-long-acting | 6 h | Flat | > 36 h | Degludec (Tresiba) |
| Premixed (biphasic) | 10–20 min | Dual | 12–18 h | NovoMix 30, Humalog Mix 25/50 |

## Common Insulin Regimens

### Basal-Bolus (Intensive Multiple Daily Injections)
- Long-acting insulin OD or BD (basal) + rapid-acting with each meal (bolus)
- Most physiological; best glycaemic control
- Dose adjustment: 1–2 units per meal based on pre-meal glucose

### Twice-Daily (BD) Premixed
- Premixed insulin given before breakfast and evening meal
- Simpler than basal-bolus; less flexibility
- Useful in type 2 DM, less complex lifestyle

### Basal-Only
- Long-acting insulin OD at bedtime
- Starting insulin in type 2 DM alongside oral agents
- Initial dose: 10 units OD; titrate by 2 units every 3 days

## Dose Adjustment — Sick Day Rules (Type 1 DM)
- NEVER stop insulin during illness (even if not eating)
- Check blood glucose every 2–4 hours
- Check urine/blood ketones if glucose > 14 mmol/L
- If ketones ≥ 0.6 mmol/L: extra rapid-acting insulin (10% total daily dose)
- DKA risk if ketones ≥ 3.0 mmol/L: hospital review

## Insulin Storage
- Unopened: store in refrigerator (2–8°C)
- In use: room temperature for up to 28 days (some up to 56 days — check product)
- Do not freeze; protect from direct sunlight and heat

## Common Errors to Avoid
- Wrong insulin (look-alike names: Lantus vs Lente; Humalog vs Humulin)
- Using rapid-acting pen for basal insulin
- Incorrect site rotation (use same region — abdomen, thigh, arm — to prevent lipohypertrophy)
- Incorrect timing (rapid-acting should be given within 15 minutes of meal; soluble 30 minutes before)

## Insulin Units Warning
- Always write "units" in full — never abbreviate as "U" or "IU" (risk of misreading as "0" or "10")
- Use insulin-specific syringes (U-100) to avoid dosing errors
