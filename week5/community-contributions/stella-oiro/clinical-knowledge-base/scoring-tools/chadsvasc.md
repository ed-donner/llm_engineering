# CHA2DS2-VASc Score — Stroke Risk in Atrial Fibrillation

## Purpose
Predicts annual stroke risk in patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation (AF) to guide anticoagulation decisions.

## Scoring Criteria
| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| **C** — Congestive Heart Failure (or LVEF < 40%) | 1 |
| **H** — Hypertension (BP > 140/90 or on antihypertensives) | 1 |
| **A2** — Age ≥ 75 years | 2 |
| **D** — Diabetes Mellitus | 1 |
| **S2** — Stroke / TIA / Thromboembolism (prior) | 2 |
| **V** — Vascular disease (prior MI, PAD, aortic plaque) | 1 |
| **A** — Age 65–74 years | 1 |
| **Sc** — Sex category female | 1 |

**Maximum score: 9**

## Interpretation and Anticoagulation Guidance
| Score (men) | Score (women) | Annual Stroke Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 1 | Low | No anticoagulation needed |
| 1 | 2 | Moderate | Consider anticoagulation (assess bleeding risk) |
| ≥ 2 | ≥ 3 | High | Anticoagulate (OAC recommended) |

**Female sex alone** (score = 1 in women, 0 in men): does NOT independently increase risk — only adds risk if another risk factor is present.

## Anticoagulant Options

### Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) — Preferred
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Apixaban | 5 mg BD (2.5 mg BD if ≥2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, creatinine ≥133) | Preferred in renal impairment |
| Rivaroxaban | 20 mg OD with evening meal (15 mg OD if CrCl 15–50) | OD dosing; good evidence |
| Dabigatran | 150 mg BD (110 mg BD if age ≥80 or gastric risk) | Avoid if CrCl < 30 |
| Edoxaban | 60 mg OD (30 mg OD if CrCl 15–50 or weight ≤60 kg) | — |

### Warfarin
- Target INR 2.0–3.0 (monthly monitoring; multiple interactions)
- Use if: mechanical heart valve, mitral stenosis, patient preference

## Bleeding Risk — HAS-BLED
Score 1 for each: **H**ypertension uncontrolled, **A**bnormal renal/liver function, **S**troke history, **B**leeding history/predisposition, **L**abile INR, **E**lderly (> 65), **D**rugs (antiplatelets/NSAIDs) or excess alcohol.
Score ≥ 3 = high bleeding risk — address modifiable factors; do NOT withhold anticoagulation for stroke prevention unless risk extremely high.
