# CURB-65 Score — Community-Acquired Pneumonia Severity

## Scoring Criteria
One point for each:
| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| **C**onfusion (new; AMTS ≤ 8 or CAM positive) | 1 |
| **U**rea > 7 mmol/L (BUN > 19 mg/dL) | 1 |
| **R**espiratory rate ≥ 30 breaths/min | 1 |
| **B**lood pressure: systolic < 90 mmHg OR diastolic ≤ 60 mmHg | 1 |
| Age ≥ **65** years | 1 |

## Interpretation and Management
| Score | 30-day Mortality | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 | < 3% | Low severity — consider home treatment |
| 2 | ~9% | Moderate — short inpatient stay or close outpatient follow-up |
| 3–5 | > 15–40% | Severe — hospital admission; consider ICU if score 4–5 |

## CRB-65 (community version — no blood tests required)
- C: Confusion
- R: Respiratory rate ≥ 30
- B: BP < 90/60
- Age ≥ 65

CRB-65 score 0: home treatment; 1–2: hospital assessment; ≥ 3: urgent admission

## Antibiotic Choice for CAP (UK guidelines)

### Low Severity (CURB-65 0–1) — Community
- Amoxicillin 500 mg TDS PO × 5 days
- If atypical suspected: add clarithromycin 500 mg BD × 5 days
- Penicillin allergy: doxycycline 200 mg day 1, then 100 mg OD × 5 days

### Moderate Severity (CURB-65 2) — Hospital
- Amoxicillin IV 500 mg TDS + clarithromycin 500 mg BD IV/PO × 5–7 days
- Step down to oral when apyrexial and improving

### High Severity (CURB-65 3–5) — Hospital (consider ICU)
- Co-amoxiclav 1.2 g TDS IV + clarithromycin 500 mg BD IV × 7–10 days
- If legionella/PVL-MRSA suspected: discuss with microbiology
- Duration: 5 days (low), 7 days (moderate), 10 days (high)

## Additional Assessment
- Blood cultures × 2 before antibiotics (moderate–severe)
- Urinary antigen (Legionella, pneumococcal) for all admitted patients
- Oxygen: target SpO2 94–98% (88–92% if COPD)
- Repeat CXR at 6 weeks (all patients) — exclude underlying malignancy
