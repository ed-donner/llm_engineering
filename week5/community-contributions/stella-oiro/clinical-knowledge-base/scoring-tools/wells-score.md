# Wells Score

## Wells Score for Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

### Scoring Criteria
| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Clinical signs/symptoms of DVT (leg swelling + pain on palpation) | 3 |
| PE is #1 diagnosis, or equally likely | 3 |
| Heart rate > 100 bpm | 1.5 |
| Immobilisation ≥ 3 days OR surgery in previous 4 weeks | 1.5 |
| Previous DVT or PE | 1.5 |
| Haemoptysis | 1 |
| Malignancy (on treatment, or treated in last 6 months, or palliative) | 1 |

### Interpretation
| Score | Probability | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 | Low (< 10%) | D-dimer; if negative, PE excluded |
| 2–6 | Moderate (~30%) | D-dimer; if positive, CTPA |
| > 6 | High (~65%) | CTPA directly (do not do D-dimer) |

**Simplified Wells (two-tier)**:
- ≤ 4: PE unlikely → D-dimer
- > 4: PE likely → CTPA

## Wells Score for DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis)

### Scoring Criteria
| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Active cancer (treatment within 6 months, or palliative) | 1 |
| Paralysis, paresis, or recent plaster immobilisation of lower extremity | 1 |
| Recently bedridden ≥ 3 days OR major surgery within 12 weeks | 1 |
| Localised tenderness along deep venous system | 1 |
| Entire leg swollen | 1 |
| Calf swelling ≥ 3 cm larger than asymptomatic side | 1 |
| Pitting oedema confined to symptomatic leg | 1 |
| Collateral superficial veins (non-varicose) | 1 |
| Previously documented DVT | 1 |
| Alternative diagnosis at least as likely as DVT | -2 |

### Interpretation
| Score | Probability | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Low | D-dimer; if negative, DVT excluded |
| 1–2 | Moderate | D-dimer; if positive, ultrasound |
| ≥ 3 | High | Ultrasound directly |

## Important Notes
- D-dimer is sensitive but not specific (elevated in infection, pregnancy, post-op)
- CTPA is gold standard for PE; compression ultrasound for DVT
- Start LMWH/anticoagulation if high clinical suspicion while awaiting imaging

## Reference
Wells PS et al., N Engl J Med 2003; NICE NG158 Venous thromboembolism 2020
