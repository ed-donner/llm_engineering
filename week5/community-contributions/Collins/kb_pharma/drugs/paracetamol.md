# Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

## What it treats
- Mild to moderate pain (headache, toothache, muscle aches)
- Fever

## Typical adult dosage
- 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours as needed
- Maximum: 4000 mg/day (many clinicians prefer ≤3000 mg/day in regular use)

## Pediatric dosage (general)
- Weight-based dosing is recommended (consult label/clinician)

## Common side effects
- Usually well tolerated at recommended doses
- Nausea, rash (uncommon)

## Serious risks / warnings
- Liver toxicity with overdose or heavy alcohol use
- Avoid combining multiple products that contain acetaminophen (common in cold/flu meds)

## Who should be careful
- Liver disease
- Heavy alcohol use
- Taking other hepatotoxic medicines

## Drug interactions (not exhaustive)
- Alcohol (increases liver risk)
- Warfarin: prolonged high-dose use may increase bleeding risk (monitor)

## Quick counseling points
- Check “acetaminophen/paracetamol” on labels to avoid double dosing.
- Seek help urgently if overdose suspected.
