# Ibuprofen

## What it treats
- Pain and inflammation (muscle/joint pain)
- Fever
- Menstrual cramps

## Typical adult dosage (OTC)
- 200–400 mg every 6–8 hours as needed
- Maximum OTC: 1200 mg/day (higher doses may be prescribed under supervision)

## Common side effects
- Stomach upset, heartburn
- Nausea

## Serious risks / warnings
- Stomach ulcer/bleeding (risk increases with higher doses, long use, older age)
- Kidney injury (especially dehydration, kidney disease)
- Increased cardiovascular risk with prolonged/high-dose use in some patients

## Who should avoid / be careful
- History of ulcers or GI bleeding
- Kidney disease, dehydration
- Uncontrolled hypertension, heart disease (use caution)
- Pregnancy: avoid in later pregnancy unless clinician directs

## Drug interactions (not exhaustive)
- Anticoagulants/antiplatelets (bleeding risk)
- Steroids (GI bleeding risk)
- ACE inhibitors/diuretics (kidney risk)

## Quick counseling points
- Take with food to reduce stomach irritation.
- Avoid combining with other NSAIDs (e.g., naproxen).
