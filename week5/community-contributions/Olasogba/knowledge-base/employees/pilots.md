# Airpeace Pilots

## Flight Crew Overview

Airpeace employs over 150 professional pilots across three fleet types, maintaining the highest standards of training and safety.

## Pilot Ranks and Requirements

### Captain (Pilot in Command)
**Requirements:**
- Airline Transport Pilot License (ATPL)
- Minimum 5,000 flight hours total time
- Minimum 1,500 hours on type
- Current Class 1 Medical Certificate
- Line check within 12 months
- Instrument rating renewal within 12 months

**Responsibilities:**
- Final authority on aircraft operation
- Crew management and coordination
- Safety decision making
- Passenger communication

### First Officer (Co-Pilot)
**Requirements:**
- Commercial Pilot License (CPL) or ATPL
- Minimum 1,500 flight hours total time
- Type rating on assigned aircraft
- Current Class 1 Medical Certificate
- Multi-crew cooperation training

**Responsibilities:**
- Assist Captain in all phases of flight
- Radio communications
- Flight planning and documentation
- Emergency procedure support

### Second Officer (Relief Pilot)
**Requirements:**
- CPL with appropriate ratings
- Type rating on B777 (long-haul only)
- Multi-crew cooperation training

**Responsibilities:**
- Augment crew on ultra-long-haul flights
- Allow required crew rest
- Support flight deck operations

## Training Programs

### Initial Type Rating
| Aircraft | Duration | Location |
|----------|----------|----------|
| B777 | 8 weeks | Dubai (Emirates Training) |
| B737 | 6 weeks | Lagos (Airpeace Training Center) |
| E195 | 5 weeks | São José dos Campos (Embraer) |

### Recurrent Training
All pilots complete every 6 months:
- Simulator proficiency check
- Line check
- Emergency procedures training
- CRM (Crew Resource Management)
- Security awareness
- Dangerous goods refresher

### Career Development
- Cadet to First Officer program (18-24 months)
- First Officer to Captain upgrade (minimum 5 years)
- Type rating transitions available
- Management track opportunities

## Flight Crew Scheduling

### Duty Limitations
- Maximum flight duty period: 14 hours
- Maximum flight time per duty: 10 hours
- Minimum rest between duties: 10 hours
- Maximum monthly flight hours: 100
- Maximum annual flight hours: 1,000

### Roster Pattern
- Domestic: 5 days on, 2 days off
- Regional: 4-day trip patterns
- Long-haul: 3-day trip with layover
