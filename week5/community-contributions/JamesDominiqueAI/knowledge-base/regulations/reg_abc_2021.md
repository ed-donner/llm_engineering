# Regulation ABC-2021: Financial Data Handling and Consumer Privacy

**Regulation ID:** REG-ABC-2021  
**Year:** 2021  
**Section:** 1 — Scope and Definitions  
**Issuing Authority:** Federal Financial Compliance Authority (FFCA)

---

## Section 1: Scope and Definitions

This regulation applies to all financial institutions operating within the jurisdiction that process, store, or transmit consumer financial data. Covered entities include banks, credit unions, insurance providers, fintech companies, and payment processors with annual transaction volumes exceeding $10 million.

**Key Definitions:**
- **Consumer Financial Data (CFD):** Any data directly or indirectly identifying a consumer's financial position, including account balances, transaction history, and credit scores.
- **Processing Entity:** Any institution that handles CFD on behalf of or directly for consumers.
- **Data Breach:** Unauthorized access, use, disclosure, or destruction of CFD.

---

## Section 2: Data Retention Requirements

Financial institutions must retain consumer data for a minimum of **7 years** from the date of last transaction. Data older than 10 years must be securely archived in compliance with Section 5. Institutions are prohibited from retaining data in unencrypted formats on consumer-facing systems.

All retained data must be:
- Encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent
- Backed up to geographically separated data centers
- Accessible within 48 hours upon regulatory request

---

## Section 3: Consumer Consent and Disclosure

Before collecting or sharing CFD, institutions must obtain **explicit written consent** from the consumer. Consent forms must:
- Be written in plain language at or below 8th grade reading level
- Specify all third parties that may receive the data
- Include an opt-out mechanism valid for 30 days after signing

Consumers may revoke consent at any time. Upon revocation, the institution has **15 business days** to cease all non-essential data processing.

---

## Section 4: Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach, institutions must:
1. Notify the FFCA within **72 hours** of discovery
2. Notify affected consumers within **10 business days**
3. Provide a written remediation plan within **30 days**

Failure to comply with breach notification timelines results in penalties of up to **$50,000 per day** of non-compliance.

---

## Section 5: Archival and Destruction Standards

Archived data must be stored in immutable format and reviewed every 3 years. Data destruction must follow NIST SP 800-88 guidelines. Institutions must maintain a destruction log for 5 years after destruction.

---

## Section 6: Third-Party Vendor Requirements

All third-party vendors with access to CFD must:
- Pass an annual security audit conducted by a FFCA-approved auditor
- Sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) before access is granted
- Report security incidents to the institution within **24 hours**

Institutions are liable for vendor non-compliance under this regulation.

---

## Section 7: Penalties and Enforcement

| Violation Type | Penalty Range |
|---|---|
| Failure to encrypt data | $10,000–$100,000 per incident |
| Late breach notification | $50,000 per day |
| Unauthorized data sharing | $25,000–$500,000 per incident |
| Failure to retain records | $5,000–$50,000 per incident |

Repeat violations within a 24-month window are subject to license suspension.
