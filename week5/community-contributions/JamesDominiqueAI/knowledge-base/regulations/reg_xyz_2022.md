# Regulation XYZ-2022: Anti-Money Laundering and Transaction Monitoring

**Regulation ID:** REG-XYZ-2022  
**Year:** 2022  
**Section:** Full Regulation  
**Issuing Authority:** Federal Financial Compliance Authority (FFCA)

---

## Section 1: Purpose and Scope

This regulation establishes mandatory anti-money laundering (AML) controls for all covered financial institutions. It applies to any institution that facilitates monetary transfers, holds consumer accounts, or provides credit products.

---

## Section 2: Transaction Monitoring Requirements

Institutions must implement an automated transaction monitoring system capable of:
- Flagging transactions exceeding **$10,000** in a single day from a single account
- Detecting structuring behavior (multiple transactions designed to evade thresholds)
- Identifying transactions to or from FFCA-designated high-risk jurisdictions
- Generating Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) within **3 business days** of detection

---

## Section 3: Know Your Customer (KYC)

All new account holders must undergo KYC verification including:
- Government-issued photo ID verification
- Proof of address dated within 90 days
- Source of funds declaration for accounts with initial deposits over $50,000
- Enhanced due diligence for Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

KYC records must be updated every **2 years** or upon any significant account activity change.

---

## Section 4: Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)

Institutions must file SARs with the FFCA Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) for:
- Transactions with no apparent business purpose exceeding $5,000
- Any transaction linked to suspected terrorism financing
- Patterns consistent with layering, placement, or integration of illicit funds

SAR filings are confidential. Tipping off a subject of a SAR is prohibited and subject to **criminal penalties**.

---

## Section 5: Record Keeping

AML-related records must be retained for **10 years**, exceeding the standard 7-year requirement under REG-ABC. This includes:
- Transaction records
- KYC documentation
- SAR filings and related investigation notes
- Staff training records

---

## Section 6: Staff Training

All staff with access to transaction systems must complete AML training:
- At onboarding (minimum 4 hours)
- Annually thereafter (minimum 2 hours)
- Upon significant regulatory changes (within 60 days)

Training completion must be logged and available for regulatory inspection.

---

## Section 7: Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Failure to file SAR | $25,000–$250,000 per instance |
| Tipping off SAR subject | Criminal prosecution + up to $1M fine |
| Inadequate KYC | $10,000–$100,000 per account |
| Failure to maintain records | $5,000/day |
