# FFCA Annual Audit Report 2022: Industry Compliance Summary

**Report ID:** AUDIT-2022-ANNUAL  
**Year:** 2022  
**Issuing Authority:** Federal Financial Compliance Authority (FFCA) — Office of Compliance Oversight

---

## Executive Summary

The 2022 audit cycle examined 847 covered institutions across all asset classes. Overall compliance improved from 71% in 2021 to **78%** in 2022. However, significant deficiencies persist in breach notification timeliness and third-party vendor management.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Breach Notification Non-Compliance (High Risk)

**38%** of audited institutions failed to meet the 72-hour FFCA breach notification requirement under REG-ABC-2021. Of these:
- 22% notified between 73–120 hours
- 10% notified between 121–168 hours
- 6% notified after 7 days or not at all

Total penalties issued for breach notification failures in 2022: **$14.3 million**

**Recommendation:** Institutions should establish dedicated incident response teams with pre-approved communication templates to reduce notification latency.

---

### Finding 2: Third-Party Vendor Audits (Medium Risk)

**54%** of institutions could not demonstrate that all third-party vendors had completed their required annual security audit as of the audit date. Common reasons cited:
- Vendor non-cooperation (31%)
- Audit scheduling delays (45%)
- Institutions unaware of sub-processor relationships (24%)

**Recommendation:** Institutions should maintain a real-time vendor inventory and include contractual deadlines for audit completion.

---

### Finding 3: Encryption Standards (Medium Risk)

**17%** of institutions were found to be using deprecated encryption standards (DES, 3DES, or AES-128) for at least some portion of their CFD storage. Non-compliant institutions were issued corrective action orders with 90-day remediation windows.

---

### Finding 4: Consumer Consent Records (Low Risk)

**12%** of audited institutions had incomplete consent records, typically due to legacy system migrations. No penalties were issued in cases where good-faith remediation efforts were documented.

---

## Positive Trends

- Data retention compliance improved to **91%** (up from 83% in 2021)
- KYC completion rates under REG-XYZ-2022 reached **96%** in the first year of enforcement
- Mobile data handling compliance (per AMD-ABC-2022-A1) reached **74%** by year-end

---

## Outlook for 2023

The FFCA will prioritize the following in 2023 audits:
1. Compliance with REG-ABC-2023 (effective January 2023)
2. AI/ADS system documentation requirements (new Section 9)
3. Privacy Impact Assessment submissions
4. Biannual vendor audit compliance

Institutions are encouraged to conduct self-assessments against REG-ABC-2023 before Q2 2023.
