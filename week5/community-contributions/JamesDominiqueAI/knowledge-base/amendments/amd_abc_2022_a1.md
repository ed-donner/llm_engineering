# Amendment 2022-A1 to REG-ABC-2021

**Amendment ID:** AMD-ABC-2022-A1  
**Year:** 2022  
**Applies To:** REG-ABC-2021  
**Effective Date:** March 15, 2022  
**Issuing Authority:** Federal Financial Compliance Authority (FFCA)

---

## Purpose of Amendment

This amendment addresses two enforcement gaps identified during the 2021 audit cycle:
1. Ambiguity in mobile application data handling
2. Insufficient guidance on biometric data as a subset of Consumer Financial Data

---

## Amendment A1.1 — Mobile Application Data

Section 2 of REG-ABC-2021 is amended to clarify that **mobile application data**, including device identifiers, geolocation logs, and behavioral analytics collected through financial institution apps, constitutes CFD and is subject to all retention, encryption, and consent requirements.

Institutions with existing mobile apps have **180 days** from the effective date of this amendment to achieve compliance.

---

## Amendment A1.2 — Biometric Data

A new sub-section is added to Section 1 (Definitions):

**Biometric Financial Data (BFD):** Biometric identifiers (fingerprints, facial recognition data, voice prints) used for authentication or identity verification in financial services contexts. BFD is a subclass of CFD and subject to **enhanced protections**:

- BFD may not be shared with any third party without explicit, separate consent
- BFD retention is limited to **3 years** (shorter than standard CFD retention)
- BFD destruction must be verified and certified by a qualified security professional

---

## Amendment A1.3 — Penalty Clarification

The penalty table in Section 7 of REG-ABC-2021 is updated to clarify that penalties are assessed **per affected consumer record**, not per incident, for:
- Unauthorized data sharing
- Failure to encrypt

This significantly increases potential liability for large-scale breaches.

---

## Compliance Deadline

All institutions must achieve full compliance with AMD-ABC-2022-A1 by **September 15, 2022**.
