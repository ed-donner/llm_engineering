# SafeHire Knowledge Base

Consolidated guidelines for hiring domestic workers safely in Kenya.

---

## Child Safety Guidelines for Domestic Workers

Domestic workers caring for children must follow basic safety standards.

### Essential Safety Practices

A nanny should:

- Never leave a baby unattended
- Follow feeding instructions provided by parents
- Maintain hygiene when preparing food
- Immediately report injuries or illness

### Supervision Expectations

Employers should:

- Observe the nanny during the first weeks of employment
- Install basic monitoring systems if necessary
- Maintain regular communication

### Warning Signs of Unsafe Care

Parents should watch for:

- Children appearing fearful around the nanny
- Frequent unexplained injuries
- Changes in child behavior

These signs may require immediate investigation.

## Verifying References for Domestic Workers

References from previous employers help determine reliability and behavior.

### Recommended Reference Checks

Employers should confirm:

- Duration of employment
- Reason for leaving
- Behavior with children
- Reliability and punctuality

### Questions to Ask References

Examples:

- How long did the nanny work for you?
- Would you hire this nanny again?
- Did you ever have safety concerns?
- How did the nanny interact with children?

### Warning Signs During Reference Checks

- References refusing to speak
- Vague answers about performance
- Contradicting employment history

At least two independent references are recommended.

## Red Flags When Hiring a Nanny or Househelp

Certain behaviors during the hiring process may indicate potential risk.

### Identity Verification Red Flags

- Refusal to provide a national ID
- Providing inconsistent identity details
- Providing someone else's identification

### Reference Red Flags

- Refusal to provide references
- Only providing relatives as references
- Previous employers unwilling to discuss employment

### Employment History Red Flags

- Frequent job changes in short periods
- Leaving jobs without explanation
- Inconsistent work history

### Behavioral Warning Signs

- Becoming defensive when asked verification questions
- Avoiding discussion about previous employment
- Changing phone numbers frequently

Employers should investigate these issues before hiring.

## SafeHire Trust Score Guidelines

SafeHire evaluates domestic workers using verification indicators.

### High Trust Indicators

A worker may be considered high trust if they have:

- Verified national ID
- Two or more references from previous employers
- Stable employment history (1 year or more per job)
- Positive feedback from references

### Medium Trust Indicators

Moderate trust situations include:

- Only one reference
- Partial employment verification
- Limited employment history

### High Risk Indicators

High risk indicators include:

- Refusal to provide ID
- No references
- Frequent job changes
- Negative employer feedback

Employers should carefully review workers who meet high risk indicators.

## Hiring Domestic Workers Safely in Kenya

Hiring a nanny or househelp requires careful verification to protect children and households.

### Recommended Verification Steps

Before hiring a domestic worker, employers should verify the following:

- National Identification Card (ID)
- At least two references from previous employers
- Duration of previous employment
- Next of kin contact details
- Consistency in work history

### Interview Best Practices

Employers should ask questions such as:

- Why did you leave your previous job?
- How long did you work for your last employer?
- What experience do you have caring for children?

### Documentation

Employers should record:

- Full name
- Phone number
- ID number
- Previous employer contact information

Keeping records helps in case disputes or safety concerns arise later.
