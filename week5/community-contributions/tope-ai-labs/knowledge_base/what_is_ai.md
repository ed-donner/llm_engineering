# What Is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the branch of computer science that aims to create machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding.

## Key Concepts

**Machine Learning** is a subset of AI where systems learn from data instead of being explicitly programmed. Instead of writing rules by hand, we train models on examples so they can generalize to new situations.

**Deep Learning** is a subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers. These networks can learn hierarchical representations of data—from low-level features like edges to high-level concepts like objects or words.

## Applications

AI is used in many domains: natural language processing (chatbots, translation), computer vision (face recognition, medical imaging), recommendation systems, autonomous vehicles, and game-playing agents. Modern AI systems often combine large datasets, powerful hardware, and sophisticated algorithms to achieve human-level or superhuman performance on specific tasks.

## Ethics and Limitations

AI raises important questions about bias, privacy, job displacement, and safety. Systems can perpetuate biases present in training data and can make mistakes in critical settings. Understanding these limitations is essential for responsible development and deployment.
